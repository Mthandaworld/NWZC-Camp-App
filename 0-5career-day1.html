Teacher’s Note

Welcome to the 2024 Camp Meeting! I hope you are as psyched as I am to be teaching the little kids. Our theme this year is “I shall not be moved”. Rightly so! We are making Christian Giants like David who will withstand the Goliaths because they are anchored and firm in the word of God.

Our lesson is based on bible characters who worked in various places. Our aim is to instill the attitude of working within our little kids at a young age. Remember the bible says, “Train up a child.”

Our activities will involve action songs and colouring pages, we hope you will do your best to bring the lesson across and make it as enjoyable and educative to the little ones.

As this lesson is for children, an arrangement has been made for their parents to have their own lesson after having a lesson involving the kids.

Be blessed and may God bless you for responding to the call to work for Him in this way.

E.T. Makuza

Day One: Mommy & Daddy and The two brothers

 

Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”

·         If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves (Parents can introduce their children when they comment)

·         Welcome all visitors

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson, children should know that God made humanity and tasked them with work and this means them too!

Into the Lesson

God made everything in the world. He made the singing birds of the air and the fish of the sea, he made the land and the beautiful trees. He made the blooming flowers, the green grass. After creating all these, God said it is good!

Sing: Who made, Animals−Jesus made the animals, I want to see a butterfly

After creating all these, God made Adam and Eve and gave them power over everything in the world! The roaring Lion, the slithering snake, the tinny winny ant, the birds in the skies, God gave them all to man. Then God put Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden and gave them an instruction. Now read the memory verse:

Memory Verse

Recite & Repeat the Memory Verse

Then God put Adam in the garden to cultivate it

(Genesis 2:15 paraphrased)

 

Colour in the image of Adam and Eve in the garden, remember, most trees have green leaves!

God made Adam and Eve and they lived together in the garden. When they went out of the garden, they had two children, Cain and Abel. Together they worked in the land and grew crops and raising sheep. Cain liked tilling the land and watching plants grow. Abel liked animals and spent most of his time looking after the sheep.

Together as a family they worked for their food and clothing. As little children, we must also help our families at home. By doing small things, we can help mommy and daddy as the little brothers Cain and Abel did.

 

Colour the picture of Abel and Cain doing what they loved most.

Action Songs: Oh be careful, He is got the whole world, Every single cell in my body

 

Activity: As a group, illustrate tasks that can be done at home.

 

Commitment: Together as a family, we will help each other with task at home.

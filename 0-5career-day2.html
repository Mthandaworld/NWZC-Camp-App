Day Two: Little Helping Hands.

 

Welcome

  • Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite
  • Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.
  • Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.
  • If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”
  • If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson, children should know that by helping at home, they are shaping themselves to be excellent in whatever career path they choose.

Into the Lesson

There were two boys who lived with their mother and father. Their father was a prophet. He borrowed money from his friend, but due to some problems, he was not able to pay back the money.

Soon, he fell ill and died, and left his family in debt. The man whom he owed came to their house and told the mother that if they did not repay the debt he was going to take the little boys as slaves. (Bible Story, 2 Kings 4

Sing: Jump Little Baby, With Jesus in the vessel, with Jesus in the family.

The woman was soooo sad! She didn’t know what to do. And then she remembered Prophet Elisha, he had worked miracles, maybe he would help her too! And indeed Elisha helped her and told her to collect as many jars as she can, once she did so, she was instructed to go home and close her doors and start pouring the oil from her own jar into the other jars she had borrowed.

So she went back home to her little boys and they went out together to look for jars. They gathered the jars and put them in their house. She closed the door and would ask her little boys to hand her an empty jar. After pouring, she would give the little boy the jar and he would put it in a safe place. Work, work, work, the little hands of the little boys went work, work, work. Little hands take the empty jar, gave it to mom, little hands took the jar from mom, and put it in a safe place! Until all the jars were full!

Activity;

Work, work, work, the little hands of the little boys went work, work, work. Little hands take the empty jar, gave it to mom, little hands took the jar from mom, and put it in a safe place! Until all the jars were full!

·         Using a rhythm, repeat the above words imitating picking the jar and giving it to mom.

·         If there are bottles they can be used as jars, and the little kids can hold them and give them to mom and mom gives them back. Repeat the exercise several times to instil into the little kids the idea of lending a helping hand.

Activity: Little Helping Hands

Requirements: Manilla or Bond Paper; Pencil or Pen, Scissors, short stick, cellotape, Crayons.

Use Manilla paper or bond paper; place your child’s hand on the paper and trace the hand’s edges. Trace both hands, use the scissors to make a cut out of the two hands.

After making the cut out, write inside the hands.

Left hand: My Name is……………………………………….. (write your child’s name)

Right hand: I will use my little hands.

With your help, colour the inside of the hand, the part you wrote.

Then tap the stick behind the little hands. Let the child hold the two sticks, and repeat together: My name is…………………………..(Shout your child’s name) I will use my little hands!

Sing: O.B.E.Y, Which side are you leaning on, Joy is the flag (Waving the little hands)

 

·         To further instil the lesson, when parents get back home they must give their children little things they want to use for their various tasks (spoons, cups) or ask to be handed things they want to use for their home tasks.

Commitment: I will use my little hands to help others at home

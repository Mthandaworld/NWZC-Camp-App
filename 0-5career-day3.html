Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”

·         If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson children should know that there is a reward for the good works they do.

Into the Lesson

Joseph had 11 brothers. Imagine having all these brothers to have fun and play with! The brothers would go out to tend sheep and sometimes they would go with Joseph. Benjamin was the youngest brother and remained behind because he was too young to go to the pastures.

Sometimes Joseph would be sent to deliver food for his brothers since they would be far away from home. However, his turn brothers would sometimes steal one of their father’s sheep and slaughter it and roast. When Joseph saw this happening, he would tell his father. Not because he wanted to punish his brothers but because he wanted them to do better. He wanted them to keep safe their father’s property, knowing that it would one day be theirs too.

But the boys didn’t stop, they kept stealing, Joseph kept telling! Their father (Jacob) decided to reward Joseph, because he wanted the others to see how good it is to be obedient and faithful. He bought Joseph a beautiful coat. It was a coat of many colours! (Genesis 37:2−3)

The Story of Achan

God told the children of Israel not to touch some things when they entered Jericho, but Achan saw a beautiful garment and he stole it. God knew about it, so when the Israelites went to war, they lost because one of them had touched God’s things. God was not happy, so He told Joshua and together they took Achan and his things and destroyed them.

·         Not being faithful angers God, so as little kids we must always be faithful, knowing that God will give us a reward. Even our parents are not happy when we are not faithful or when we steal sugar.

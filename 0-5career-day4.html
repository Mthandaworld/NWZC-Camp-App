Day Four: The Little boy with a Small lunch

Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”

·         If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson, children should know that giving and sharing pleases God

Into the Lesson

One day a little boy woke up very early in the morning. He wanted to go and see Jesus! He had heard that Jesus was coming to their area and he wanted to see Him and listen to his word.

His mother prepared lunch for him, a small lunch with 5 loaves of bread and two fish. He went with his lunch to see Jesus and he saw Jesus! Jesus preached and healed and cared for those who were not feeling well. He preached from the morning until it was in the afternoon, and then until it was almost sunset. People’s stomachs started to grumble and rumble, they were hungry. Jesus knew that the people were hungry but there was not enough food to feed them all. He asked the disciples but they too didn’t have any food. The little boy overheard their conversation and he brought his food, all of it, to Jesus.

Sing: Every single cell in my body, with Jesus in the vessel, we are his children, He’s got the whole world.

The people were not happy, they were hungry, but because Jesus was around, they should not have worried. Jesus took the food from the little boy, asked the people to sit. Then he prayed for the 5 loaves and two fish and they multiplied!

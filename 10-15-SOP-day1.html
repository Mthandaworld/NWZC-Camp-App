How to teach the lesson

The lesson has six sections, each section is necessary for the lesson to be fully understood.

1.                  Memory Verse

The memory verse of the day covers the theme of the day. Memorising it enables better understanding of the whole lesson.

2.                  Objective of the Lesson

This section covers what the lesson is aiming achieve.

3.                  Content

This section covers details about the lesson. It might be a Bible passage, a story or basic points of the lesson that the children need to know. Covering the content makes the objective of the lesson clearer to the students.

4.                  Songs

This section has songs that help the children grasp the concepts of each day’s theme.

5.                  Activities

Fun, exciting and catchy activities, appropriate for each lesson, are used to help the children to understand the lesson. The activities help children commit the lesson to memory.

6.                  Commitment

The aim is for the children to commit themselves to the objective of the lesson.

 

 

DAY ONE: “SPEAK LORD, I AM LISTENING”

Explain to the children what a prophet is:

·                   Prophet – A person who speaks for God.

·                   What is a Prophet?  A Prophet is a person who speaks to people on behalf of God.  He is God’s messenger to the people.

MEMORY VERSE

1 Samuel 3:9 New Living Translation (NLT)

“Speak Lord your servant is listening.”

 

LESSON OBJECTIVES:

At the end of the lesson, children should know that:

1.                  God Has a work for everyone.

2.                  It is good to obey God.

3.                  We can hear and respond to God’s voice.

STORY:

Today our topic is, “Speak Lord, I am Listening?”

1. Hannah’s story:

Samuel’s mother is where our story begins, she was barren and Peninah, Elkanah’s second wife always made her life miserable because of this.  So, one day while at the temple she prayed that God could bless her with a child and that if He did, she would give the child back to Him. Priest Eli thought she was drunk but that did not stop her from asking God for the blessing.  And she received the blessing Samuel and as promised she took him to the temple.

2. Samuel’s call:

One evening while Samuel was sleeping God called out his name.  this happened three times and he kept going to Eli thinking it was him who was calling, until Eli told him it must be God calling.  He instructed Samuel to respond, “Speak Lord, your servant is listening.”  Samuel did and that began his prophetic ministry.  God told him that He was not happy with what the children of Eli were doing and that they would die in battle.  If you were Samuel, would it have been easy to tell Eli the message?

3. Eli’s sons:

Priest Eli had sons who were notorious for their bad behavior.  And in this environment was Samuel, he turned out to be different because of the choices he made.  The environment we find ourselves in may be bad, but that is not an excuse.  We can still make right choices as did Samuel. 

4. Positioning oneself to hear God’s voice:

There is so much noise around as with Samuel, the noise of Priest Eli’s sons was loud but it did not stop him hearing God’s voice.  Today there is so much noise in social media, in our schools and the places we visit.

ACTIVITIES

1. Who Is Calling Me?

Instructions:

  1. Have all players sit in a line.
  2. Choose one player to come to the front and sit with their back to the other players.
  3. The players in the line should take turns calling out the name of the player at the front while trying to disguise their voices.
  4. The player at the front must then guess who called their name. If they guess correctly, the player who called their name goes to the front and the game continues.
  5. If the player at the front guesses incorrectly, the game continues with the next player in line calling out the name.
  6. The game can go on as long as you like, giving each participant the opportunity to be at the front.
  7. Discuss Samuel’s experience of recognizing God’s voice and call.

2. Remember Your Promises

Instructions:

  1. Have all players sit in a circle.
  2. The first player will begin by making a promise. For example, “I promise to brush my teeth.”
  3. The next player must then repeat the previous player’s promise and add one of their own. For example, “I promise to brush my teeth and make my bed.”
  4. The game continues with each player repeating all the promises in the exact order and adding a new promise.
  5. If a player forgets a promise or says them out of order, they are out of the game.
  6. The last person remaining who can correctly list all the promises is the winner.
  7. After the game, discuss the importance of remembering and keeping our promises to God, just like Hannah kept her promise to God.

3. Prayer Journal Creation

Materials needed: Notebooks, pens, markers, stickers, coloured pencils.

Instructions:

  1. Encourage each person to decorate their notebook.
  2. After the decorating is done, ask each member to write their first entry. This could be a prayer request, a note of gratitude, or a reflection on the Bible study of Hannah and Samuel.

Discuss the importance of keeping a prayer journal as a way of remembering God’s faithfulness, similar to how God remembered Hannah.

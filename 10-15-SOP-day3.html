Memory Verse

Daniel 6:27 New International Version, “He delivers and rescues; He works signs and wonders in heaven and on earth, He who saved Daniel from the power of the lions.”

Lesson Objectives

At the end of the lesson, children should know that:

1.                  God sends angels to watch over us.

2.                  Pray always and do not be afraid.

STORY:

Daniel was a prophet who had been taken from his country and was now living in a country where the people had many idols. In this country Daniel and his friends remained faithful to God and did not worship idols. One day the King had a dream which he could not understand and God helped Daniel tell the King what the dream meant.  The other leaders in the country did not like Daniel because of this and they decided that he should die.  Now you see Daniel talked to God through prayer three times a day and these men knew that the only way to get to Daniel was through his God.  They tricked the King into saying anyone worshiping any other God but him would be put to death, s/he would be thrown into the Lion’s Den. Oh Noooo! Do you think Daniel stopped praying? No he did not and because of that he was thrown into the Lion’s Den.  The King was so disturbed and could not sleep at all that night early in the morning he rushed to the Lion’s Den, imagine his joy when he saw Daniel alive and well in the Lion’s Den.  God’s Prophet Daniel went through a scary situation but God watched over him.  Daniel followed God’s law which went against the king’s laws even it meant that he would be in trouble or even killed.  He needed to be brave and courageous and trust that God would protect him even when things seemed terrible.  Throughout our lives there will be times when things are difficult and we might feel alone.  God promises to be with us always as He was with the prophets of old.

Daniel was able to assist us today with prophecies on what will happen throughout the ages as w await Christ’s soon return.  Teacher can share some of Daniel’s prophecies e.g. Nebuchadnezar’s Dream Daniel Chapter 2

ACTIVITIES:

1.         Daniel in the Lion’s Den

This activity develops gross motor skills while helping children internalize the theme of the story. Play the game “London Bridge” in the usual fashion but substitute the following verses for the traditional words:

  • Daniel’s in the lion’s den, lion’s den, lion’s den. /Daniel’s in the lion’s den, trusting God to save him.
  • Angel with Daniel in the den, in the den, in the den. /Angel with Daniel in the den, sent from God to save him.
  • Angel shut up the lion’s mouth, lion’s mouth, lion’s mouth. /Angel shut up the lion’s mouth, sent from God to save him.

2.         Prayer buckets

Turn a large container into a mini prayer bucket. Have the child draw someone or something they want to pray for on a square piece of paper. After they draw the person or thing they want to pray for, direct them to drop it in the prayer bucket. Try to schedule three time a day the child should pick an item to pray for from the bucket. An easy way would be, breakfast, meal-time and before bed.

3.         Roar like a Lion

One person faces the wall. Everyone else stands somewhere in the room behind them.

The people behind take it in turns to roar like a lion. The person facing the wall must try and guess who is making the roar.

CONCLUSION:

Say, Daniel trusted God, how can we show that we trust God? Let the kids give their answers. Other possible answers would be that they should not be afraid to do right even if no one else is doing right.

STORY:

Ellen and her twin sister Elizabeth were born on November 26, 1827, to Robert and Eunice Harmon.  She was the seventh of eight children.  At age 9, she suffered a near-fatal accident when a school girl threw a stone at her and hit her in the face breaking her nose.  This resulted in her terminating her formal education, making her a sickly child. In 1840, intrigued by Baptist preacher William Miller's "Advent awakening" revival preaching, Ellen gave her heart to Jesus. And was baptised in the Methodist church, on June 26, 1842.  She was deeply disappointed when Jesus did not return in 1843 and again in October 1844 as predicted by Millerites.  At age 17 (December 1844), she experienced her first of many visions.  Ellen married evangelist James White in 1846 and they accepted the Seventh day Sabbath.  Ellen and James had four sons, the youngest died when he was three months and the eldest died when he was 16 years old. Ellen had over 2000 visions during her lifetime and it is said that she did not breath during the visions, and her eyes were always open without blinking.  She has written many books.

When she was called, she felt inadequate for the job but was willing to allow God to use her.  At times we may also feel inadequate when God call.  Let us be willing to respond “yes Lord, here I am use me.”

Read up on Ellen G White and share interesting facts about her.

ACTIVITIES:

1.         Red light, Green light

Play the game all line up at a starting point. The goal is to be the first one to reach the end point — or finish line. When the leader says “Green!” every player runs. As soon as the leader yells “red” every player has to stop…immediately.  If anyone moves, they are OUT and cannot continue the race. We even yell “yellow” to change things up for a super slow walk.

2.         Follow the Leader

How does this teach kids to obey?  It teaches them to copy whatever the leader is doing. If the leader claps, they clap.  If the leader hops, they hop!  A classic game that teaches how important it is for everyone to follow the leaders in their life!

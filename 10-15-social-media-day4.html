Cyberbullying and Its Psychological Effects

 

Cyberbullying: includes any abuse or harassment by groups or individuals such as abusive messages, lies, stalking or spreading rumors.

 

Social media platforms have unfortunately become breeding grounds for cyberbullying. Behind the anonymity of a screen, individuals feel emboldened to engage in hurtful behavior. Cyberbullying can have severe psychological effects, leading to increased levels of anxiety, to emotional distress, social isolation, and even suicidal thoughts.

 The relentless nature of online harassment can leave lasting scars on one’s mental well-being.

 

Annoying content:

 When a person interacts with content on social media, the algorithm is designed to show more similar content. Social media platforms have guidelines to keep inappropriate content off their websites.

Social media can still include inappropriate content, such as:disturbing or violent content or sexual or obscene content. This includes a wide range of content that annoys, upsets or irritates, such as disturbing or violent content or sexual or obscene content. Dangerous challenges. In fact, in one survey, a majority of teens reported accidental exposure to pornography online.

 

How to Avoid

  1. The best policy is to not ask for and not send inappropriate images or texts.
  2. Be careful about the accounts you follow and who you message.
  3. Don't click suspicious links.
  4. Block or unfollow accounts that post inappropriate content.

 

 

Sharing Lies

Most of the stories and information that are shared on social media are not true stories. When these stories are read, some think that these stories are true. A lot of people are misled to believe a lie. It is important to check a story before you share it. Sometimes the writes of the story will put a misleading story headline or Topic. These fake stories are there to have you to click. Incorrect information can be very dangerous and negative. It is important that you avoid sharing information that you are not sure of. Some people may make important health decision based on lies. Some may purchase wrong health products because you would have shared fake information.

 

Misleading Buying and selling

Social media is filled with ads and sponsored content for products, services, apps, and more.

Misleading advertising is different than outright scams. Companies and social media marketers want to make money. Sometimes this means they use dishonest marketing strategies, including:

  • Fake or biased reviews
  • False product promises
  • Not disclosing sponsorships
  • Photoshopping images
  • Fake time-sensitive deals

To avoid being misled by adverts, look out for Buzz words and Flash Sales.

 

Scams

There are people on the internet that want to steal. They create stories or situations so that you can give them money. These fake stories or situations are usually touching and moving for you to help. Once you try and help the people by sending digital money or digital bank details, they then steal all the money that is in the account. Some scam projects promise that they can make you rich quick, if you invest a small amount. The most common ones are the ones that want to send you money. If it sounds too good to be true, it might be a scam. Always be cautious, ask questions, and do extra research before spending or sending money online.

 

Dangerous Challenges

The internet makes it seem like you can hide your identity. It offers risky offers like doing a criminal activity, dangerous pranks, drug abuse and sexual experimentation. These challenges seem to have a financial benefit if you participate in them. These challenges are made to be valued for breaking rules and going against bible principles. Be picky about what accounts you follow on social media. Think twice before recording and posting risk-taking behaviours

 

Privacy Issues: the constant sharing of personal details and location updates on social media can compromise one's safety, making individuals vulnerable to cyberstalking and real-world threats. Social media platforms have access to vast amounts of personal information, which raises concerns about privacy and security. Data breaches and instances of personal information misuse have become increasingly common, putting users at risk of identity theft, fraud, and other malicious activities.

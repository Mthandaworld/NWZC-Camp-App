Digital Ministry

 

the Great Commission in this new digital context. Digital ministry allows access to places and people that have never been reachable before, and we want you to be a part of reaching people with the gospel. That means they have access to the gospel all day, every day—usually in a familiar language. Digital strategies allow the gospel to reach the hardest places to reach, including locations where it is not possible to physically send missionaries.

 

Focus of Digital Ministry Online.

The main focus of digital ministry is not to flight or share church services and church events. The main focus is to reach out to people that are not yet in the Adventist Church. The focus of the lessons should to inspire hope in a hopeless world. This is why Its important to train churches on exactly how to run their social media, to make it not complicated or time-consuming. 

I firmly believe that your church, regardless of its size or tech-knowledge, can use social media to connect with a community in a meaningful way. Instead of treating social media as a virtual bulletin board, the idea of stewardship utilizing the resources given to us by God to serve others and help people know Him. Social media isn't just about broadcasting information; it's about genuinely engaging with people and offering something to serve and help.

 Every church has a unique voice, a distinctive expression, and a potential to reach individuals only they can reach in their local context.It is important that the people that run and administer social media are part of the local church. Even if churches decided to hire people from outside for social media management, It remains crucial for local church to have internal oversight. The purpose of social media is to create relationship. We have to We are to be a letter that everyone reads. We have to influence people to know the goodness of the Lord.

There are different programs that we could create and share online.

Devotionals:

This will come in the form of someone reading a devotional from a book or someone sending in a 3-5 minute video devotional about how the Lord has encouraged or helped them lately.

 

Testimonies:

These are 3-5 minute videos we’ve asked people to make to share a part of their testimony or a testimony of how the Lord is helping them during a difficult time. Here, we also include thank you videos to pastors, soldiers, doctors and the nurses in our country

 

Children’s videos:

 These include videos with songs, Bible verses and stories, dramatized stories and puppet shows. We also have videos where someone is reading different children’s stories/lessons or out of a children’s Bible.

 

Interviews:

We can interview with people that help grow our faith. It could be a life story of faith of a church member or even pastor.

 

Biblical Study:

We are sharing a Bible verse per day and are encouraging people to memorize that verse and meditate on it. We also have a lesson study of our sabbath school lessons.

 

 

 

 

Steps to a successful Digital Ministry

WE ENGAGE - In the presentations or content request for people to share their experience in the comment section. When people ask questions, we have a online Chaplain to answer those questions and provide material to help the people who may need answers.

 

WE EMPOWER -In the presentations or content, empower people to practice the principles that are being shared. Give testimonies of the goodness of the Lord in all things. All content should be less of entertainment and more of life changing topics or discussions.

 

WE LEAD – Online ministry is not a church, its small part of church. All presentations should lead people to a physical church gathering near them. It should always lead people to Baptism and true repentance.

 

WE COLLABORATE – the Church has atleast 23 ministry departments, it is important have a collaboration of all departments in content creation.

 

Digital Tools that can be used at church.

 

Virtual Platforms

 it becomes clear that the online classroom can be done. Over Zoom Meeting App or any other such app. Distance should not be a reason why people cannot meet or interact in services or discussions.

 

Texting

Texting may almost seem too personal, but it’s practical, as most of us check our texts frequently. Apps like WhatsApp and Facebook allow you to easily communicate as a church or children’s group, even scheduling texts ahead of time in coordination with your lessons. Texts are also more reliable than take-home papers which often get left behind.

 

Use of Projectors / Big TVs and LED Screens

Humans are visual beings. Most people process information better when they are looking at illustrations. It is also important that visual can be used for lessons and sermons. There are digital software to present Hymnals and Bible verses.

 

Interactive Polls and Quizzes

People understand truth and information when you ask them questions. There is a new technology that can be used to display questions and people give feedback within a lesson or sermons. There are several Polls and Quiz sites that can be used. The preacher or presenter can share a Poll and people share their answers. This helps to keep the church members engaged in the service.

 

Digital breakout rooms

Instead of members of one church to move churches and places, they can digitally break into smaller groups, called digital breakout rooms. These rooms will enable the discussions in each group to be recorded and saved for future use.

 

Bible Based Games.

the seventh-day adventist church has developed a bible based game that can help sharpen your Bible knowledge, Challenge yourself to use power-ups to beat your best time, Discover the stories of the great heroes of the Bible. Challenge your friends and family to answer the same questions you just answered.

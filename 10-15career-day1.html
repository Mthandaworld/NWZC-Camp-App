How to teach the lesson

The lesson is divided into 7 divisions, each of these divisions are necessary for the lesson to be understandable. You can either cover them individually or weave them into each other.

1.      Memory Verse

·         The memory verse of the day covers the theme of the day. By memorizing it, we come to a better understanding of what the lesson entails.

2.      Objective of the Lesson

·         This covers what the lesson is aiming at, by the end of the lesson, its objective must be understood.

3.      Content

·         This segment covers the whole lesson subject. It might be a bible passage, a story or basic points of the lesson that the children need to know. By covering content, we make the objective of the lesson clearer to our students

4.      Know, Feel, Respond

·         This segment covers questions, examples and comments that resonate with the lesson and helps children to apply the lesson to themselves

5.      Reflections

·         This gives the lesson’s highlights. Particular attention is given to parts of the lesson that better gave meaning to the lesson objective and their impact on the children.

6.      Activities

·         Fun, exciting and catchy, some of the activities help children in understanding the lesson. Activities help children commit the lesson to memory. The activity makes the children link to the lesson

7.      Commitment

·         Our aim is for the children to commit themselves to the objective of the lesson.

Into The Lesson

Today our topic is, “What shall I do?”

At Creation, God made all things beautiful, He made sure that everything came in the correct order so as not to distort things. Whatever was created each day had the capacity to sustain whatever was gonna be created the following day. One such example is that of the earth and trees, imagine if God had created trees first, where would they anchor their roots? Nowhere! But God ensured that everything was done in its order.

 

This poses a question to all of us, are we doing things in their order? I believe the eldest in our class must probably be 15 years old, this means we are still learning, most probably be Form 2 or Form 3, this means our focus is most likely supposed to be schooling but some of us have other things we are already doing besides school. This means we are no longer doing things in their order, instead, it's now haphazard and will thus result in confusion. We must look at the beauty of the creation week and yearn to have our lives beautiful and in such an order that's heaven sent. Don't rush into anything, wait the time is coming.

 

So having created everything, God created man last, and gave him dominion over everything, furthermore, he gave the man the garden to tend it and all the animals to name them. And the Bible states that whatever name Adam gave, became the name of that creature. (Genesis 2:15, 19)

As someone who was created with everything, Adam would have probably asked the question, "What shall I do?"

God in His wisdom would have named the animals Himself, and He sure was very much capable of tending the garden, but He left all these in Adam's hands because He didn't expect to have a puppet Adam who was ever idle. He wanted Adam to work, even in that perfect world Adam had to work.

Some of us come from rich families where everything is provided, we must not relax and think that since everything is there, there is no need to work. If Adam, the first man created by God, had everything he needed but was still given something to do by God, what more of us? We must not shy from work but we must do various tasks to help either at home or at school.

 After sin, they then left their home and were banished from that garden..... God increased their work, it wasn't tending the garden anymore, it was now hard-core labour, and Adam was told that he would eat by sweat. And sweat he did to eat! (Genesis 3:17, 19).

Each of us has a dilemma, what am I supposed to do. Some of our parents up to now are not so sure if what they chose was the ideal career for them. But the bottom line is, you are supposed to work! What you shall do is another issue but the main issue is you must work.

There are some among us who shy away from labour and give excuses to be excused from working, we are digging our own graves. We are sowing seeds of poverty

Read Proverbs 6:6-11

What do these verses say and what do they teach us?

So we have to imagine Adam in the garden, and see him tending the flowers and looking after the trees. By his wit named he the animals, and he did that work and finished it. Most people often start something but do not finish it. So in every instance, we must always remember that God requires us to work.

Recite & Repeat the Memory Verse

“For even when we were with you, we commanded you that if anyone doesn’t want to work, he must not eat.” (2 Thessalonians 3:10)

We can find practical examples from biblical families, beginning with Cain and Abel, while the focus has always been on how Cain murdered his brother, we have often overlooked the undeniable fact that they were both labourers with their parents... that's why at the time of offering they both brought something, Abel as a shepherd brought a choice lamb from the flock and Cain brought vegetables from the garden as a farmer.  They both understood labouring, and they worked hard in whatever field they were in.

One other such family was the family of Noah, building the ark wasn't an easy task, especially considering that this huge building was supposed to house all the animals of the world. But as a family they worked together and they managed to finish the work that had been set before them. Imagine if maybe Shem or Ham had decided that they would rather watch soccer, would they have achieved anything within the stipulated time? So by their collective effort they managed to finish the work that had been set before them.

Take note that for them working was a life and death situation, without finishing that work it meant they too would be wiped out by the flood. As we work, we must always remember that we are fleeing poverty, Jesus says whoever lays his hand to the plough must not look back, we must work and see whatever we are doing to fruition.

 

Another family we cannot ignore is the family of Jacob, when they entered Egypt, they were asked what they do and they responded that they were shepherds. Despite being related to the governor, they still wanted a piece of land where they would settle and work. So they were given the land of Goshen and they stayed there with their flocks. Jesus was a carpenter, Paul was a tent maker, Matthew was a tax collector, and Peter was a fisherman, even though they were called to another work, they too had their own professions and somewhere they were occupied.

 

There is a great relationship between labouring and the Sabbath. The verse itself says, six days shall you Labour and do all your work, it explicitly tells us that for us to rest there must be labouring first, and not just labouring but labouring to completion we must do all our work before the Sabbath arrives.

Someone once said, laziness is resting without having laboured.

If we just rest but without having worked then we have missed the mark. Ecclesiastes also says whatever your hand find to do, do it with all your might..... So the bottom line is we must work.

 

Reflections

1.       God created man and gave them things to do, we must find work to do

2.       We must plan and do everything at the right time

3.       We must be dedicated to the work, if possible, gaining expertise on it and seeing to completion

4.       We must be identified with what we do

5.       We must rest after having worked, that's the only way to enjoy the Sabbath

6.       Even if we decide to work for God, He must take us from labouring somewhere.

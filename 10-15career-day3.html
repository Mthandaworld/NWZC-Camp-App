It is in the Bible for people to look for skilled labour, and it is in the Bible that God bestows skill on some people. Today we shall look at gaining skills for employment. Our economy has become so polarised to the extent that there are so many things that one can do as long as they are trained for it. However, I noticed something, we have dream jobs which we talk about when we are still young, and these jobs are usually the ones we pursue when we are at school. The reason why we choose to take sciences, arts or commercials is because we want to pursue a certain job that is closely linked to the faculty we chose. But did you know that sometimes you can be very passionate about doing something but not passionate in doing it? God knows where your passion is and sometimes He leads you to it.

 

Into the Lesson: A Short Story

When people asked Pride about what he wanted to be when he grew up, when he was around 4 or 5, he used to tell them he wanted to be a doctor. At the time, he was living with his grandfather then, and his grandfather wasn't feeling well. So he was his grandfather’s help, his nurse aid. Every task that involved taking care of him, he performed it with the help of his aunties and uncles. So because the situation at the time required doctors, he would become a doctor.

But when he was around 6 to 8 years, he went through some troublesome time and felt that the person troubling him deserved to be locked up in prison. So for some time he wanted to be a policeman. He only had one target and that was to imprison this one person. After he healed from those situations, he reverted back to the initial desire, which was to be a doctor.

That's when another terrible setback came up, he was very much terrible at mathematics but he was so well invested in reading, reading books like 'Tunzi the faithful shadow' and ‘Mpho's’ Search before the age of 10. He never found time for mathematics, crunching numbers were not his thing. Yet nobody told him you cannot be a doctor without mathematics.

 

Grade 7 he passed with flying colours though he had 2 units in mathematics, he enrolled at a certain school and at that school, he only managed to get 2 examination papers of his end of year examination. In Shona, he had scored 90% but in Mathematics he had 4%. This streak remained and he kept failing this subject, yet the dream of being a doctor didn't die down.

When he was form 2, he actually asked for biology text books from his form four friends and kept the dream on. At form 3, they were asked to choose a class, his subjects spoke for themselves that he must go to the arts class, but the system considers science students as best, commercials as medium and arts students as lowest class. So he went for best with his doctor's dream in his pockets.

He struggled those two years with physical science (chemistry and physics) and barely made it in biology yet always failing mathematics.... what made him part of the stream's top ten was excelling in arts subjects. But the doctor's dream remained in his mind and he even dreamt of opening a surgery.

The final O level exam was the one that finally told him the truth that he is an art student.... He passed all other subjects and failed the three that are meant to propel someone to advanced level in the science class. Biology, physical science and mathematics, that's when he did Arts at A Level and passed them.

If Pride had chosen to do Arts when he was form three, don’t you think he would have passed them with flying colours? Because the times he spent learning and studying things he didn’t understand would have been spent better with those he understood.

From this story alone, we can draw a couple of lessons which can help us see what we can do when choosing a career path. For starters, choosing a career path is not about looking for money, but finding what you are very passionate about. You also have to consider if your education matches your passion. You might very much be passionate about selling things, yet in accounts and commerce flanking the subjects while excelling in science. You also have to consider the demands of the field you are choosing, whether they will continue fostering your relationship with God and you also have to consider if the dividends of that particular field would make you afford the lifestyle you desire.

 

1.       Passion

It is the drive that gives you the desire to do something. Without it, it is difficult to navigate life. For example, there are many people who are being trained for the health sector either as nurses or nurse aids. However, some of them are not passionate about helping people, they are passionate about the opportunity to go abroad. So when you are not passionate about caring for people, your work will be affected because you won't give the 100% care and effort that is required for a particular task. You have to choose a career path that will always make you smile, and that leaves you contented. There are some people who work until they are tired out but you can see the glimmer in their eyes when they are describing their work. You can clearly see that despite the exhaustion, this person is happy with what he/she is doing. Choosing something you are not passionate about makes you miserable while also jeopardising the wellbeing of your workmates and your clients.

2.       Skill

While passion is crucial, it should be accompanied by the skill to do the task at hand. For those who have chosen to study a certain field, you have to compare the field with your current studies. If you can, take your report card from time you started form one, if you just started you can take your marks in mock tests. Add them and then average them by dividing the marks by the number of tests you wrote.... if for example you realise you are excelling in sciences and failing in arts, it’s most likely that your field of expertise should have something that involves Science subjects. Once you have identified that, you can then consider which field in the science sector you want to pursue. If you love animals, you might have to consider being a vet or venturing into farming. And if you talk too much or with a desire to help others mentally, you might consider becoming a lawyer or counsellor.

3.       Relationship with God.

Whatever you choose as your career, you have to research whether it won't jeopardise your relationship with God. Remember, while we can work, our sole duty while on earth is to fear God and keep His commandments. So we have to ensure that our work aligns with our sole duty.  There must be no contradiction and God shouldn't compete for space in our lives. Consider the career, research on it and see if won't affect your relationship with God. When considering your career, pray about it and God will rouse you and give you a path that will be perfect for you.

4.       Salary

While all these are crucial, they don't beat the pleasure of just knowing that at the end of the month you are getting a salary that befits the work that you put up at work. You have to get a salary that sustains you and your family and helps you cater for your needs.

 

After having put these things into consideration, you must prayerfully ask God to guide and lead you into making the right choice when it comes to employment. Choose employment that glorifies the Lord and also makes you happy for the rest of your life. Remember, choosing employment is similar to choosing a life partner, if you make a mistake you will regret it for the rest of your life.

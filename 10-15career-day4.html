Into the lesson

Today we shall look at another aspect of employment which is called self-employment

Â·         What does this word mean?

         i.            Self-employment is defined in the dictionary as working for oneself. This means you are your own boss

 

While employment is the most viable option being taught in schools, we must also consider being self-employed. The biggest advantage of being self-employed is in the freedom that it provides. Today we shall look at what one would need to consider when considering self-employment

Capital

If you are considering self-employment then you have to see if you have enough capital. There are some who are self-employed in the fields they studied for example, Doctors with surgeries, lawyers with private practices, psychologists with counselling centres, IT technicians with their own companies. These probably started working for others then saved up enough money to start their own companies.

So when considering self-employment you have to know exactly what you need.

Then there are some who venture into business like selling wares, you also have to know how much you need to start whatever you want to do. Capital is the money that is raised to start a business.

 

This money is raised by many means, one of which is to save up until the required amount is reached. The other is to secure a loan, which is not recommendable as you don't know whether the business will succeed or not.

When looking for a loan from some individuals or a bank or a moneylending organisation, itâs important to note they might ask for a business proposal that highlights exactly how you are going to use the money you are given. This business proposal will include where you shall get whatever you want, how you shall distribute it, when you shall return the money and at what percentage.

 

Company Registration

After raising the capital, you have to also consider registering yourself or your company with the responsible authority. There are perks associated with being a registered company. Most registered companies prefer to work with organisations that are registered. You must also consider being registered with PRAZ (Procurement Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe) this allows you to be viable for tenders that are posted by the government or big organisations like ZESA, City Councils, Polytechnics and universities. You must also have a tax clearance certificate from ZIMRA (Zimbabwe Revenue Authority) which is up to date. This shows that you are faithful in paying taxes. Being self-employed doesn't exempt you from the duty you have to your country through payment of taxes and the like.

Location

After having done setting up your company, you need to secure a busy location for whatever you want to do... for example, it's more recommendable to sell sweets by the school gate as compared to opening a butchery there.

Advertisement

After securing a location, you will need advertisement. Due to the influx in technology, some now do their advertisements online, while others make posters, flyers and banners, there are some also who have audio advertisement on loud speaker, for example 'mari dzakabvaruka tinotora'

Building a Client Base

After getting customers, you have to be consistent in whatever you are supplying, you have to build a relationship with your clients and create a database so that you can always get in touch with them when you have other things to offer. The most important thing to consider is to offer the best service that gives the customer the need to come back to your organisation for the same service, when they think of doing the same thing, they must think only of you before seeking other options. Have good communication skills, smiling and being open with clients. Even when there are issues affecting you, donât be aloof and cold towards clients, you lose them.

Financial Management

The trickiest part in being self-employed is financial management.  The reason why most entrepreneurs fail is because they end up spending everything, profits and capital included because they don't have financial regulation. You have to set a clear boundary on your income and never dip your fingers in the capital jar. Even the profits, some of them should be kept for expansion.

Some of the advantages of self-employment are

1. Self-management. You are in control of yourself you don't answer to anyone thus you have to manage yourself

2. It fosters creativity.  In a company some of the creative ideas you have can be shot down by management but as an entrepreneur you get to explore creative ways to boost your business or the services you offer.

3. Time regulation. You are in control of your time, this means you can close for Sabbath or other religious programs like zundes, efforts, and camp meetings

4. Creates employment. If you expand your business, you create employment for others

 

The most viable option that you can choose is to pursue self-employment. If you are going to be a skilled labourer and need to be employed first then use that employment to gain experience.

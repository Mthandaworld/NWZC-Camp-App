How to teach the lesson

The lesson has six sections, each section is necessary for the lesson to be fully understood.

1.      Memory Verse

The memory verse of the day covers the theme of the day. Memorising it enables better understanding of the whole lesson.

 

2.      Objective of the Lesson

This section covers what the lesson is aiming achieve.

 

3.      Content

This section covers details about the lesson. It might be a Bible passage, a story or basic points of the lesson that the children need to know. Covering the content makes the objective of the lesson clearer to the students.

 

4.      Songs

This section has songs that help the children grasp the concepts of each day’s theme.

 

5.      Activities

Fun, exciting and catchy activities, appropriate for each lesson, are used to help the children to understand the lesson. The activities help children commit the lesson to memory.

 

6.      Commitment

The aim is for the children to commit themselves to the objective of the lesson.

LESSON  ONE: OH DEAR! WHAT HAPPENED?

Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         Our slogan for Evangelism lessons is as follows:

-          Leader shouts “Evangelism”

-          The Children shout back “Evangeliving”

-          Leader shouts “Evangelism, Evangeliving”

-          The children shout back “I am a messenger for Jesus”

 

Explain to the children that:

·         Evangelism means telling others about Jesus.

·         Evangeliving means letting the way you live preach about Jesus. (e.g. the way you talk, the way you dress, what you eat, what you watch, what you listen to and the games you play.

Repeat Campmeeting 2024 Theme

-          Leader shouts “Campmeeting 2024”

-          The children shout “I shall not be moved. Faithful till the end.”

 

If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If the classes are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, Bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

 

MEMORY VERSE

Genesis 1:31 New Living Translation (NLT)

“Then God looked over all he had made, and he saw that it was very good!”

 

LESSON OBJECTIVES:

At the end of the lesson, children should know that:

1.      In the beginning, God created a perfect world. Everything was very, very good.

2.      Adam and Eve disobeyed and caused the chaos that is there today.

 

CONTENT

Today our topic is, “Oh dear, what went wrong?”

1.      In the beginning, God created a perfect world. Everything was very, very good. Give examples of the scenery that was there: The trees were always green, fruits trees always had fruits, flowers did not wither, trees did not shed their leaves, the grass was nice and soft, there were no deserts, there were no floods, it never used to rain-water used to come up from the ground and water the plants like a mist, there were no thorns.

 

2.      There was peace and harmony in the newly created earth. All animals were very nice and they all were friendly to human beings. Adam and Eve used to play with the animals. All animals were very tame, animals did not each other nor did they attack `human beings.

 

3.      Life in the garden of Eden was very exciting. There was no sickness. There was no getting tired. There was no getting thirsty or hungry. There was all the food that Adam and Eve needed. All the food was natural and very healthy. There was no cooking to be done. Adam and Eve ate all food in its natural, very healthy and nutritious form. Adam and Eve did not know anything called fear. They were always very safe and happy. There was no fighting, no telling lies, no stealing, no shouting, no getting angry, no grumpiness, no temper tantrums, no hatred. There was no sadness, no crying, no pain, no sneezing, no coughing, no headaches, no sweating, no itchiness, no death in Eden. What a wonderful and lovely place it really was. Wouldn’t you like to be in a place like that?

 

4.      So, what caused things to change from very good to terrible? The answer is just one word. Would you like to guess what that one word is?

Yes, very correct. The answer is DISOBEDIENCE!!!

Adam and Eve knew that what they were doing was wrong. God had forbidden them from eating the fruit in the middle of the garden. Adam and Eve had also been warned not to entertain Satan.

 

Question: Why are we disobedient at school and at home? Why do we sometimes do what we have told not to do? Let’s discuss. Allow the children to contribute as much as possible, allow about 5 to 7 minutes of discussion as a class.

 

In the case of Adam and Eve, the enemy, Satan, was not the problem. The big problem was that Adam and Eve decided to disobey God’s command. God had given them a test of obedience and they failed the test.

 

Romans 5:19 New Living Translation “Because one person disobeyed God, many became sinners”

 

5.      As a result of Adam and Eve’s disobedience, the earth started suffering. Trees and plants started to wither, Adam and Eve started sneezing, coughing, feeling pain, becoming sick, tired, hungry thirsty, angry, moody and …… Help me name the other terrible results of Adam and Eve’s disobedience. Allow the children to contribute as much as possible, allow about 5 to 7 minutes of discussion as a class.

 

This first lesson should make it very clear what has made Evangelism and Evangeliving necessary.

i.)                 Man’s disobedience to God

ii.)               Ever presence of the enemy, the Devil

LESSON FOUR – SUPER HEROES

 

Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         Our slogan for Evangelism lessons is as follows:

-          Leader shouts “Evangelism”

-          The Children shout back “Evangeliving”

-          Leader shouts “Evangelism, Evangeliving”

-          The children shout back “I am a messenger for Jesus”

 

Repeat Campmeeting 2024 Theme

-          Leader shouts “Campmeeting 2024”

-          The children shout “I shall not be moved. Faithful till the end.”

 

Recap of Previous Lesson, key highlights:

1.      We are ambassadors of heaven.

2.      No matter how young I am, God can use me to tell others to choose and follow God.

1.      I can tell others about God through preaching, by providing food and clothes to the needy, by helping the elderly and by the way I live.

 

Memory Verse

 

Lesson Objectives

1.      Jesus is the one and only True Super Hero!

 

 

CONTENT

Who is your favourite Super Hero? What can he/she do?

Please name the other Super Heros that you know. Allow children to name the Super Heros they know. Afterward, tell them that you will show them some super heroes that you know.

Ask the children to identify the Super Heros in the picture.

How many talk to their friends about their favourite Super Heros? I see that all of you enjoy talking to your friends about super Heros.

All the super heroes we have mentioned look very different from each other and have different type of powers, but they all have one thing in common, they all save people!

Tell me something, are those super heroes you seen in movies and on your lunch boxes, your T-shirts, are they real? Can they fly and do they really have the power that they show in the movies? Absolutely, NO! It’s all fiction. None of it is true.

Now, let me tell you of some Super Heros in the Bible.

1.      Moses. What did he do? Let the children answer, then mention any of the following which they will not have mentioned:

a.       Moses led the children of Israel out of Egypt

b.      Moses parted the Red Sea for the children of Israel to cross

c.       Moses made water come out of a rock

 

2.      Joshua. What did he do? Let the children answer, then mention any of the following which they will not have mentioned:

a.       Joshua made the sun stand still

3.      Elisha. What did he do? Let the children answer, then mention any of the following which they will not have mentioned:

a.       Elisha makes an axe head float on water.

4.      Samson. What did he do? Let the children answer, then mention any of the following which they will not have mentioned:

a.       Samson killed a lion

b.      Samson carried the city gates over the mountain

5.      David: What did he do? Let the children answer, then mention any of the following which they will not have mentioned:

a.       David killed Goliath

 

BUT HERE IS THE GREATEST SUPER HERO EVER

Would you like to guess who it is? YES, IT’S JESUS

 

6.      JESUS

Jesus has more superpowers than Superman, Batman, and Spiderman and a zillion other superheroes combined. In fact, Psalm 33:9 tells us that God simply spoke, and the universe appeared!

Genesis 1:3 says that God just called out “Let there be light!” and, BOOM, there was light! Bet you can’t do that. I know I’m too lazy even to get out of bed and turn on the light in the morning, so it sure would be nice to just speak and make things happen.

God is super powerful, and he created you and me.

Something else that is awesome about God is that He is omnipresent meaning that He is everywhere at the same time. There is no place in all created space where He is not found at any time.

Jesus protects and keeps us all safe. Is that not wonderful? Oh yes, it is.

Jesus also wants each of us to be a super hero for Him. Would you like to be Jesus Super Hero? How can you be one of Jesus Super Heroes? Do you want to know? Here is what Jesus says about us being His super heroes.

Ephesians 6: Put on the full armour of God, so that you can take your stand against the devil’s schemes

We should wear God’s armour every day for us to be really protected against the devil. Here is what this armour stands for:

1.      Helmet of Salvation.

2.      Sword of the Spirit which is the Word of God, The Bible, which we should read every day.

3.      Sheild of Faith.

4.      Breastplate of righteousness.

5.      Belt of truth.

6.      Shoes of the gospel, ready to tell others about God.

7.      Praying always for power from Jesus.

 

It is important that we tell others about Jesus as our Super Hero. We also need to tell them that Jesus has given us ammunition to use against the devil. This is really good news, is it not?

COMMITMENT

I will always have Jesus as my one and only Super Hero. The armour of God I will put on everyday where ever I may be. I will tell other of Jesus as my Super Hero and urge them to make Him their Super Hero as well.

 

 

PRAYER

Dear Lord Jesus, thank you for being my one and ONLY Super Hero. Please help me tell others about your powers and your willingness to save me, my family and all my friends. In Jesus name I pray, Amen!

How to teach the lesson

The lesson has six sections, each section is necessary for the lesson to be fully understood.

1.                  Memory Verse

The memory verse of the day covers the theme of the day. Memorising it enables better understanding of the whole lesson.

2.                  Objective of the Lesson

This section covers what the lesson is aiming achieve.

3.                  Content

This section covers details about the lesson. It might be a Bible passage, a story or basic points of the lesson that the children need to know. Covering the content makes the objective of the lesson clearer to the students.

4.                  Songs

This section has songs that help the children grasp the concepts of each day’s theme.

5.                  Activities

Fun, exciting and catchy activities, appropriate for each lesson, are used to help the children to understand the lesson. The activities help children commit the lesson to memory.

6.                  Commitment

The aim is for the children to commit themselves to the objective of the lesson.

 

 

DAY ONE: “SPEAK LORD, I AM LISTENING”

Welcome

·                     Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·                     Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·                     Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

Explain to the children what a prophet is:

·                   Prophet – A person who speaks for God.

·                   S/He is God’s messenger to the people. 

·                   What is a Prophet?  A Prophet is a person who speaks to people on behalf of God.

Repeat Camp meeting 2024 Theme

-          Leader says “Campmeeting 2024”, “I shall not be moved. Faithful till the end.”. Sing the song “I shall not be moved”, with the children.

 

Know the names of the children in the class, if the class is small.

MEMORY VERSE

1 Samuel 3:8 -9 New Living Translation (NLT)

A third time the Lord called, “Samuel!” And Samuel got up and went to Eli and said, “Here I am; you called me.”  Then Eli realized that the Lord was calling the boy.  So Eli told Samuel, “Go and lie down, and if He calls you say, “Speak Lord your servant is listening.”So, Samuel went and lay down in his place.

LESSON OBJECTIVES:

At the end of the lesson, children should know that:

1.                  God communicates with us in many ways.

2.                  No one is too young for God to use.

3.                  We must listen carefully when God speaks to us.

STORY:

Today our topic is, “Speak Lord, I am Listening?”

Samuel was the son of Hannah. Hannah wanted a baby boy very much for a very long time and when God gave her Samuel, she was very very happy and she made sure that she kept her promise to give him back to God. So, he was raised in the temple where he helped Priest Eli. One night when Samuel was sleeping, he heard someone call his name, “Samuel, Samuel” and he thought it was Priest Eli so he jumped up and ran to Priest Eli (Sing: Priest Eli did you call, did you call, did you call. Priest Eli did you call, Eli did you call me).  Samuel said to Priest Eli “Here I am”, “I did not call you replied Priest Eli. Go back to bed.” So, Samuel went back to bed. Again, the LORD called, “Samuel!” Samuel jumped out of bed and went to Priest Eli (Sing: Priest Eli did you call, did you call, did you call. Priest Eli did you call, Eli did you call me).  “I didn’t call you, go back to bed,” Priest Eli answered a second time.  A third time God called Samuel and Samuel got up and went to Priest Eli (Sing: Priest Eli did you call, did you call, did you call. Priest Eli did you call, Eli did you call me). And Samuel said: “Here I am!”

Finally, Eli realized that it was God who was calling Samuel. He told Samuel, “Go and lie down, and if he calls you again, say, ‘Speak LORD, your servant is listening.”
 Samuel went back to bed and sure enough, again he heard the voice of God calling, “Samuel! Samuel!” This time Samuel answered as Eli had told him, “Speak Lord, for your servant is listening.”
 God spoke to Samuel and told him many things that would happen soon. 
 God spoke to Samuel and was able to use him even though he was young. We need to listen for God just like Samuel did. The message God gave to Samuel was a difficult one and at first, he was afraid to tell Priest Eli, but then he did tell him.  At times God gave prophets very hard messages but the message had to be delivered.  God gives us hard messages too at times as His disciples, we should be like Samuel and say it as God instructs us.

ACTIVITIES

1.                  Come Here Samuel Game
Have the class sit in a circle. One child will lay down in the middle of the circle and pretend to be asleep. Pick a child in the circle to say, ‘Come Here, Samuel.’ Samuel will then wake up and guess correctly then the two children will switch places and the game continues. If the child guesses incorrectly then you will say, ‘Go back to sleep’ and someone else will say ‘Come Here Samuel.’ Continue until they pick the correct child.
Say, When the Lord called for Samuel, he thought it was Eli at first. Eventually he realized that it was the Lord calling him. The Lord had an important message for Samuel.  

2.                  Make Telephones 
Give each child two tins (Baking Beans Tins) or paper cups. Poke a hole in the bottom of each tin/cup and connect them with a string or ribbon about 3 ft. long. Secure the string inside the tin/cup with tape. Decorate the cups with crayons or stickers.  Show the class how to talk through one end to a friend.
Say, When God called Samuel, He didn’t use a cell phone! He spoke directly to Samuel. We need to always be listening for signs that God is speaking to you.  

3.                  Listening Game
Have the kids sit with their heads bent down and their eyes closed. Make different sounds and have the kids try to guess what the sound is. You can use a smart phone to do this by looking up sounds on YouTube or items in the classroom such as knocking on the door, a timer, or crinkling paper.
Say, You did a good job listening today! Remember that you can always listen to God!

4.                  Blindfold and follow the voice
Have one child blindfolded and then another child to call out to him or her.  The child with the blind fold should try and go the one calling them focus on that voice and follow the voice till they get to the caller.  The caller should move around and to make it more interesting you can add two or three more “callers”.

Say, you did a good job listening for the right voice today! Remember that you can always hear God’s voice!

COMMITMENT

CONCLUSION:

Say, Samuel learned that it is very important to listen to God. How can we listen for God? Let the kids give their answers. Other possible answers would be that they should read their Bible and listen or God and listen when they come to church.

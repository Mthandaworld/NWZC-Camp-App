Social Media and Us

Many kids today don't speak just Shona, Ndebele, Tonga, Chewa, or English. They also "speak" social media! Friends used to hang out in person, but now we also meet and talk online a lot. Social media is very powerful and can change how we feel and what we do. That's why it's super important to learn about both the fun stuff and the not-so-good stuff about it. We need to be careful and smart about how we use social media.

What is Social Media?

Social media is a way to use the internet to share ideas, stories, pictures, and videos with people all over the world. It helps us connect with friends, family, and even new people in different places.

 

Social Media Sites : Tik Tok, WhatsApp, YouTube Instagram, Facebook, Twitter(X),Pinterest, Reddit, Twitch, Quora, WeChat, LinkedIn and many more.

 

It is important to understand that technology is neither some scary, evil plot to destroy their minds and souls, nor is it the heaven-sent answer to all of earth’s problems. It’s just a tool, and it can be used for good or evil. Like everything else in life, it comes down to the choices we make. We should learn to make good and informed social media choices in our own lives

 

Social media has changed the way we live, and it's a big part of our world now. It has some good things, like helping us connect with friends and family who live far away, sharing ideas and information, and even spreading positive messages.

 

Many churches, including SDA churches, use social media to share God's word and messages. We can use social media to:

 

- Keep in touch with friends who move away or live far away (Proverbs 18:24)

- Do research for school projects or work assignments (Proverbs 1:7)

- Support our favorite Christian music artists and learn about their music (Psalm 100:2)

- Discuss hobbies and spiritual topics with people around the world (Matthew 28:19-20)

- Share our thoughts and feelings with others who care about us (James 1:19)

 

The internet has a lot of potential for good! But it's important to remember what God wants from us. He wants us to flee from evil desires and pursue righteousness, faith, love, and peace (2 Timothy 2:22). He wants us to think about things that are true, noble, right, pure, lovely, and admirable (Philippians 4:8).

 

Our minds and bodies belong to God, and we should use them in a way that honors Him (1 Corinthians 6:19-20). Let's focus on things that bring us closer to God and make our lives better, not worse. We can do this by:

 

- Following Christian accounts and pages that inspire us (1 Thessalonians 5:11)

- Sharing our own thoughts and experiences about God's love (Psalm 96:3)

- Connecting with others who share our values and beliefs (Hebrews 10:24-25)

- Using social media to learn about God's world and His creation (Psalm 24:1)

- Being kind and respectful to others online, just like we would in person (Ephesians 4:32)

 

Let's create activities that help us remember this, and it will help us stay away from unhealthy obsessions. We can:

 

- Set limits on our screen time and take breaks from social media (1 Corinthians 9:27)

- Read our Bibles and pray before bed instead of scrolling through our phones (Psalm 119:105)

- Create art, music, or writing that expresses our love for God (Psalm 150:3-5)

- Spend time in nature and appreciate God's creation (Psalm 19:1-4)

- Talk to our parents, pastors, or youth leaders about our struggles and questions (Proverbs 1:7)

 

God wants us to live in harmony with His principles and to take care of our bodies and minds. Let's use social media and the internet in a way that brings us closer to God and makes our lives better, not worse (Romans 12:2)."

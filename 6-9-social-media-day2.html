Day 2

The Thief of Time.

 

Memory Text

 

Ephesians 5:16(ESV) - making the best use of the time, because the days are evil.

 

Objectives

1.    The Children Should Know Stealing time is Stealing Life

2.    The Children should Feel Social Media Steals life.

3.   The Children should Respond making a commitment to re-gain control of their lives from social media.

 

Character Word: Self Control

What is self control?

How do I practice self control?

"Time is like a special gift, and how we use it matters! Just like how we celebrate our birthdays, our lives are made up of moments in time. When we waste time, we're actually wasting a little bit of our life.

 

It's important to be careful how we use our time, especially when it comes to things like social media and the internet. These can be fun tools, but they can also be tricky and make us forget what's truly important.

 

The Bible says, "Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom" (Psalm 90:12). This means we should make the most of our time and use it wisely.

 

Some people get caught up in things online that aren't good for them, but God wants us to be smart and make good choices. He says, "Don't copy what the world does, but let God change you from the inside out" (Romans 12:2).

 

Social media can be fun, but it's important to remember that it's just a tool, and it's not more important than our relationship with God and the people around us. Let's make sure we're using our time in a way that makes God happy and helps us grow into kind, wise, and amazing people!"

The Invisible Secrets of Social Media

 

 The Goal is to win the Competition of social media

"Did you know that there are many social media sites and apps out there, like games and tools to help you be more productive? But here's the thing: you can only use one app at a time! All these apps are like kids in a classroom, trying to get your attention.

 

Some social media sites even have special workers called Attention Engineers. Their job is to make sure you stay on their app for as long as possible! It's like they're trying to keep you in a fun house, but it's not always good for you.

 

Imagine you're playing a game, and it's really fun. But then, you get a notification from another app, and it's like a friend calling your name. You want to see what's up, so you switch apps. Then, another app notifications pops up, and it's like another friend calling your name! It can go on and on like this, and before you know it, you've spent hours switching between apps.

 

Remember, it's important to be in control of how you use your time and apps. Don't let the apps control you!"

 

"Be careful! Some social media apps have sneaky tricks to keep you stuck on them for a long time. Here are some things to watch out for:

 

- "You might also like..." suggestions that keep you scrolling

- Ads that seem to know what you've been talking about

- Fake friends who want to chat with you instead of your real friends

- "Breaking news" alerts that try to grab your attention

- Live videos that aren't really important or useful

- Notifications that pop up even when you're not using the app

- Comment sections that try to keep you arguing or engaged

- Ads that promise easy money or riches

- Counters that show how many likes, shares, or views something got

- Celebrities and influencers who post lots of content to keep you hooked

- Infinite scrolling that never ends!

- Notification sounds that can even wake you up!

- Videos that play automatically, one after another

- Full-screen content that takes over your whole screen

- Bright and colorful designs that try to grab your attention

 

Remember, it's important to be aware of these tricks and use social media wisely!"

 

I hope this helps kids be more mindful of how social media apps are designed to keep them engaged!

 

The Goal is to promote addiction

Did you know that some social media apps are designed to be like a big puzzle, trying to keep you stuck on them for as long as possible? It's like a game, but not a fun one! They want to make you addicted, so you keep coming back for more.

 

Imagine someone trying to trick you into playing a game that you can't stop playing. That's kind of what's happening with some social media apps. They use tricks like the ones used by people who want you to spend money on games or gambling.

 

These tricks are designed to keep you hooked, like a fish on a hook! They want you to keep scrolling, liking, and watching, so you stay on the app for a long time. But it's important to remember that too much screen time isn't good for you.

 

You're smart and can make good choices! Be aware of these tricks and use social media wisely. Take breaks, play outside, read a book, or do something creative. Your brain and body will thank you!"

 

The Goal is to disturb you

"Imagine you're playing with building blocks, and someone keeps knocking them down. That's kind of what apps do to your focus! They try to disturb you on purpose, so you get distracted and spend more time on their app.

 

When someone or something disturbs you, it takes a long time (like 23 minutes!) to get your focus back. Apps know this, so they try to disturb you in many ways, like:

 

- Notifications that pop up out of nowhere

- Alerts that make you wonder what's happening

- Messages from friends that you feel like you need to answer right away

- Fun videos or games that you just want to watch "just one more time"

 

The more they can disturb you, the longer you'll stay on their app! But it's important to remember that you're in control. You can close the app, take a break, and come back to what you were doing. You're strong and can stay focused!"

 

 

The Goal is to make money

Did you know that social media apps are free to use? That's because you're not the customer, you're the product!

 

Think of it like a toy box. The toy box is free, but inside, there are toys that have your name on them. Those toys (your information) are what the toy box (social media app) sells to other people (advertisers).

 

When you use social media, you're giving them information about yourself, like:

 

- What you like (your favorite things)

- Where you go (places you visit)

- What you talk about (your messages)

- What you want to buy (things you search for)

 

They use this information to show you ads that they think you'll like. It's like they're trying to guess what toy you want next!

 

Remember, it's important to be careful what you share online, and to only use social media apps that your parents or guardians say are okay."

 

The Goal is to advertise the Soft Life.

Hey kids! Do you know how sometimes TV and social media show people living a life that seems perfect and easy? That's because they're trying to sell you a dream called a "soft life". But the truth is, life isn't always easy. Everyone faces hard times, no matter how much money they have.

 

Money can make life more comfortable, but it can't fix everything. It can't make sad days disappear, or make mean people be kind. It can't even keep us from getting hurt or feeling brokenhearted.

 

The people on TV and social media might seem happy and perfect, but they're not telling you the whole story. They might be hiding behind a mask, and they might be struggling just like everyone else.

 

So, what can help us through the hard times? Jesus Christ is our hope and our savior. He loves us no matter what, and He can help us through even the toughest days.

 

Remember, life might be hard, but with Jesus, we're never alone. And that's the truth!"

 

 

The Goal is to steal your Time

Did you know that social media can be a sneaky trap? It wants to keep you busy for as long as possible, so you spend more time watching videos, scrolling, and playing games.

 

Instead of doing important things like:

 

- Reading your school books

- Reading your Bible

- Helping with chores

- Focusing in church

 

You might get distracted and spend your time on social media. This can steal your time away from God and important activities.

 

Remember, God wants us to use our time wisely and honor Him. Let's be careful not to get trapped in social media and forget about what's truly important.

 

Let's make a choice to use our time wisely, listen in church, an

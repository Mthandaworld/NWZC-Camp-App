THE BAD MIRROR.

 Do you know how sometimes we look in the mirror to check how we look? Some people look in the mirror to make sure they look great, and they feel happy and confident. But others look in the mirror and compare themselves to others, and that can make them feel sad, worried, and unsure.

 

Comparison can take away our joy! And social media can be like a tricky mirror that makes us compare ourselves to others even more. We might take lots of pictures to find the perfect one, but the truth is, we're amazing just the way we are! We don't need to compare ourselves to others or try to look perfect like in a picture.

 

Social media can make us feel bad if we don't get likes or comments, but remember, that doesn't change who we are! We're special and loved no matter what. Let's be kind to ourselves and others, and not compare ourselves too much. We're all unique and amazing in our own way!"

 

 

 

BRAND BULLYING

Do you ever feel like you need to have something just because your friends have it too? Like a certain toy, game, or clothes? That's called peer pressure. And sometimes, people buy things just to show off to others. But here's the thing: it's okay to be different! And it's not okay to bully someone just because they don't have the same things as you.

 

Imagine you're playing with your friends at recess, and they're all wearing the latest sneakers. You might feel like you need to have those sneakers too, or else you won't fit in. But remember, your friends like you for who you are, not for what you're wearing!

 

Or maybe you see a commercial on TV for a new toy, and all your friends are talking about it. You might feel like you need to have that toy too, or else you'll be left out. But remember, there are lots of fun toys and games out there, and you don't need to have the exact same thing as everyone else to have fun!

 

Sometimes people might feel left out or sad if they can't afford the cool things that others have. But remember, your worth and awesomeness come from who you are, not from what you have! And it's important to be kind and respectful to everyone, no matter what they have or don't have.

 

Let's not fall for the trap of thinking that we need certain things to be happy or cool. Let's be happy with what we have, and let's be kind to everyone, no matter what! We can play together, share what we have, and have fun without needing to have the same things. And if someone is bullying you or making you feel bad because of what you have or don't have, remember that you can always talk to a trusted adult, like a parent or teacher, for help and support."

 

LIES BEHIND THE SCENES

Do you know that not everything you see online is true? Some things are made up or fake, like cartoons or movies. And sometimes, people make themselves look different or better online using filters or special effects. It's like wearing a costume!

 

Social media is like a big show where people share only the best parts of their lives. They might show you a picture of a yummy cake they ate, but they won't show you the messy kitchen they made while baking it! Or they might post a picture of themselves on a fun vacation, but they won't show you the long plane ride or the sunburn they got.

 

It's important to remember that everyone's life is different, and it's okay if yours doesn't look like someone else's. Don't compare yourself to others, because it's like comparing apples and oranges! Instead, be happy with who you are and what you have.

 

And guess what? Some people online are pretending to be someone they're not, or showing you things that aren't real. It's like a big game of dress-up! They might use fake names, or pretend to be someone famous. They might even use someone else's pictures or videos and say they're their own!

 

So, always be careful when you're online. Remember that things aren't always as they seem. And if you're feeling sad or unhappy because you're comparing yourself to someone else, take a step back and remember that you're amazing just the way you are!

 

Let's be happy with our own lives and not compare ourselves to others. We're all special and unique, and that's what makes us awesome! And if you ever need help or someone to talk to, don't forget that you can always reach out to a trusted adult, like a parent or teacher."

 

THE DEVIL’S PULPIT

Did you know that everything we see, hear, or watch can teach us something? It's like a big lesson, or a sermon! But sometimes, that lesson might not be a good one. Satan, the bad guy, likes to mix a little bit of truth with a lot of lies. He wants us to believe things that aren't true, and do things that aren't good for us.

 

When we watch movies, videos, or TV shows, or listen to music, we need to be careful. Some of those things might have bad messages, like jokes or words that encourage us to do things that aren't right. Those messages can be like a sermon for the devil!

 

Even things that seem fun or harmless can have hidden messages that aren't good for us. So, we need to be careful and think about what we're watching or listening to. Is it glorifying God, or is it promoting something bad?

 

Remember, nothing is completely innocent. Satan likes to hide his messages in things that seem okay, so we need to be wise and careful. Let's be smart and choose things that honor God, and avoid things that don't!"

 

ALONE IN A CROWD.

 

Do you know why social media can be tricky? It's because the people who made it know that we all want to feel loved, special, and connected. They want us to think that we can get that from their site or app. But it's not always real!

 

They might suggest friends or connections that you might know, and when you add them, it feels like you're connected to their lives. It's like feeling like you know a celebrity just because you follow them online! But it's important to remember that it's not the same as having real friends and family who care about you.

 

When we spend too much time on social media, we might start to ignore the people around us, like our friends and family. We might start to think that our online friends are more important than the people who are really there for us. But that's not true!

 

Let's be careful and remember that real connections are made in person, not just online. Let's not forget to pay attention to the people who care about us and are there for us every day!"

 

FEAR OF MISSING OUT

Do you know why we like social media? It's because we want to feel like we belong to a group of people who like the same things as us. We already belong to a family, a church, a school, and a group of friends, but social media makes us feel like we're part of something bigger.

 

Social media is always updating us on events and programs that are happening, and they usually look like a lot of fun! But sometimes, if we can't participate or don't know what's going on, we might feel like we're missing out. And that can make us feel like we don't belong.

 

But here's the thing: it's okay to miss an event! It's okay not to know what's going on all the time. Life is not just about events and programs, it's about living and enjoying the world around us. And life goes on whether we know what's happening or not.

 

So, let's not worry if we miss something on social media. Let's focus on the people and things that are really important to us, like our family and friends. We already belong to a lot of amazing groups, and that's something to be proud of!"

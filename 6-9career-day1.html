How to teach the lesson

The lesson is divided into 7 divisions, each of these divisions are necessary for the lesson to be understandable. You can either cover them individually or weave them into each other.

1.      Memory Verse

·         The memory verse of the day covers the theme of the day. By memorizing it, we come to a better understanding of what the lesson entails.

2.      Objective of the Lesson

·         This covers what the lesson is aiming at, by the end of the lesson, its objective must be understood.

3.      Content

·         This segment covers the whole lesson subject. It might be a bible passage, a story or basic points of the lesson that the children need to know. By covering content, we make the objective of the lesson clearer to our students

4.      Know, Feel, Respond

·         This segment covers questions, examples and comments that resonate with the lesson and helps children to apply the lesson to themselves

5.      Reflections

·         This gives the lesson’s highlights. Particular attention is given to parts of the lesson that better gave meaning to the lesson objective and their impact on the children.

6.      Activities

·         Fun, exciting and catchy, some of the activities help children in understanding the lesson. Activities help children commit the lesson to memory. The activity makes the children link to the lesson

7.      Commitment

·         Our aim is for the children to commit themselves to the objective of the lesson.

Day One: Mommy & Daddy and The two brothers

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson, children should know that God made humanity and tasked them with work and this means them too!

Our lesson this week shall focus mainly on work. As we grow older, it shall be required of us to work. As soon as you finish school, your parents and guardians expect you to find something to do. However, you cannot not just wake up and say ‘I wanna be a doctor’ without first working towards being a doctor. You cannot wake up and say ‘I want to be a farmer’ without first working towards becoming a farmer. The time to work towards becoming what you want to be is NOW!

The choices you make today determine what you shall be when you grow up.

As we explore this lesson during the course of the week, we shall focus on some bible characters who understood the idea of work and what they did to pursue that idea. By learning their experiences, we too can learn how to conduct ourselves when it comes to doing our duties and we can do them faithfully, knowing that we are following in the footsteps of our Bible Friends.

Into The Lesson

Today our topic is, “Mommy & Daddy and two brothers”

At Creation, God made all things beautiful, He made sure that everything came in the correct order so as not to distort things. Whatever was created each day had the capacity to sustain whatever was gonna be created the following day. One such example is that of the earth and trees, imagine if God had created trees first, where would they anchor their roots? Nowhere! But God ensured that everything was done in its order.

 

So having created everything, God created man last (Genesis 1:26), and gave him dominion over everything (vs27), furthermore, he gave the man the garden to tend it (Genesis 2:15) and all the animals to name them (Genesis 2:19). And the Bible states that whatever name Adam gave, became the name of that creature.

We can actually see Adam and Eve looking after the garden as God had instructed them.

Ask: What do you think they were doing as a way of tending the garden?

·         Probably they were trimming the trees beautifully

·         Sweeping the pavements so that the garden stays clean

·         Weeding out the little weeds

·         Doing everything to make sure that the garden looks perfect.

 After they sinned, they then left their home and were banished from that garden..... God increased their work, it wasn't tending the garden anymore, it was now hard-core labour, and Adam was told that he would eat by sweat. And sweat he did to eat! (Genesis 3:17, 19).

When they were no longer in the garden, they had their children, two little boys. Do you know their names? (Cain and Abel)

Thus Adam and Eve lived with their two little boys Cain and Abel. God had told Adam that he would eat after labouring, thus he taught his two boys to work. They would till the land together and plant seeds. When it was time to weed the land, Adam would take Cain and Abel and they would do it together. Adam also had some wonderful sheep and cattle, and they would also look after them together.

The little boys continued to grow and as they grew older, they began to show their father what they liked. Abel enjoyed looking after the sheep. He would pick the little lambs that were newly born and carry them home, if a sheep was injured he would look after it and nurse it to recovery.

Cain enjoyed tilling the land, he liked to see plants grow. He would plant his seeds and wait for them to grow in the land. He would cheerfully weed out the little weeds that wanted to keep his plants from growing. He was so good at farming that his crops were ever green!

Thus Mommy and Daddy and the two brothers worked at home for their sustenance

Did you know?

·         Adam and Eve are called our first parents!

·         What Cain and Abel were doing was their professions

·         They followed what they enjoyed doing when they were young and did it when they had grown up.

·         These professions helped them look after themselves, the sheep and the crops were like a salary after working.

·         They had food and clothing from their work and took some to give to God (even though Cain gave the wrong gift of course, he should have given Abel some of his crop in exchange for a lamb to offer as sacrifice)

 

Reflections

1.       God created man and gave them things to do, we must find work to do

2.       We must help at home just like Cain and Abel

3.       We must find something to do which we like very much, this is our profession

4.       By helping at home, we might actually find something to do for the rest of our lives

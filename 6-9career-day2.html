Day Two: Charity Begins at Home.

 

Welcome

  • Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite
  • Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.
  • Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.
  • If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”
  • If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson, children should know that by helping at home, they are shaping themselves to be excellent in whatever career path they choose.

 

Recap of Previous Lesson, key highlights:

  1. God created man and gave them things to do, we must find work to do
  2. We must plan and do everything at the right time
  3. We must be dedicated to the work, if possible, gaining expertise on it and seeing to completion
  4. We must be identified with what we do
  5. We must rest after having worked, that's the only way to enjoy the Sabbath
  6. Even if we decide to work for God, He must take us from labouring somewhere.

 

Introduction

If you read your bible well, you will discover something interesting, Jesus started his ministry at home! (Luke 4:16) While he was a carpenter, his ministry started in the synagogue where he was brought up, where they knew him and had grown up with him. He probably had assisted with making the benches they were sitting in Church.

 

Some of the people in church had either helped him at some point or been helped by him. Among those in the congregation were some friends he had gone to school with, and also some who had come often to the workshop. He didn't start with people who don't know him, he started with those that do.

 

If ever we will achieve anything in life, we must understand that everything starts where we stay. We cannot gain popularity outside for doing great things when our families don't know those things about us.

 

Into the lesson

This means that when it comes to working, the first employer is our parents or guardians. They are the ones who give us the first tasks to do and often with the instruction on how to do it. Our ability to follow the instruction and do the work as stipulated then propels us to greater heights outside our homes and beyond.

Every tree is sustained by the roots, it can soar high and bear much fruit but without roots that tree can easily fall down. If the tree didn't dig its roots deep enough and spread them wide enough it wouldn't sustain the weight of the fruits....  when the winds and storms come, it won't withstand the pressure and can easily be wiped off. For us, our roots are obviously our homes, if we don't dig deep at home, it's gonna be hard to succeed in a foreign land. We must work at home before we are hired by someone else...

 

Helping at home begins in the room we sleep in. Many of us if we go back to our tents right now, the blankets haven't been spread, clothes from yesterday are by the door, wet towels next to our dirty shoes, we probably didn't bother closing the tent and if it hadn't been for our parent or guardian, that tent would still be open by now.

 

The way we handle our things at home tend to reflect on how we will handle things in life. If we don't have discipline at home it is difficult to be disciplined in life because it will be something foreign to us.

The next thing that shows our capability in life is our ability to be given tasks and to do them diligently and cheerfully. Anyone who loves instruction and knows how to follow it shall prosper. There are many among us who are given tasks to do but sometimes we don't do them or we do do them yet they are half cooked.

Read Proverbs 13:18 and comment on it….

The Bible clearly says if we are faithful over little things we shall be made overseers over much (Matthew 25:21). The 'little things' are the menial tasks we do at home. When our parents give us something to do and we do it with all diligence, God sees this and gives us authority over more. So by being faithful to duty at home and doing it willingly, we open avenues of greater responsibilities in the future.

Let's take a glimpse at the parable of the talents, the one where they were each given talents according to their abilities. Those who multiplied their talents were awarded with those talents and there were invited to 'enter into the joy of your father' but he who had buried his talent had his talent taken from him and given to another. If you are faithful over little, God will make you oversee plenty.

It might seem like fairy-tale, but if we try it we shall see the results. Don't make your parents yearn for an obedient daughter or son when you are there. Help them and see what God will do for you.

The greatest joy comes from doing tasks that are mostly done at home yet without being told. Don't wait for the garden to be parched before your father says water the garden. The moment that you are reminded of a task that is meant for you to do, the moment it becomes burdensome. You start seeing your parents as nagging people of which they are not. So take joy in doing tasks, and take pride in doing them without having been reminded about them.

 

The other thing we must always bear in mind is that we must work at home, not because we want the reward, but because we want to please our parents and we want to fulfil a duty that has been bestowed upon us. Don't be faithful over little because you want to oversee plenty, be faithful over little because it is your duty to do so. When the reward comes, it is an extra benefit to the things you are already doing. God knows your heart, He knows the reason behind whatever you are doing and too often when you work for reward a reward rarely comes. The servants of the talents didn't know that the master was gonna give them more, they worked faithfully and the master rewarded them for that of his own volition. This made the reward more enjoyable because it was unexpected. So if you do tasks awaiting reward, you will be surprised when no reward comes.

Recite & Repeat the Memory Verse

“For even when we were with you, we commanded you that if anyone doesn’t want to work, he must not eat.” (2 Thessalonians 3:10)

The greatest gift that you can ever receive from anyone, is the 'thank you' that comes from you parents or your guardians. That simple thank you with a smile can take you places you never thought you will ever be in. Your parents' thank you is a blessing which will propel you to greater heights.

Don't shy away from labour at home, work with your hands, plant flowers, water the garden, clean the house, tend the cattle, wash clothes, do all tasks you possibly can and enjoy the blessings from your parents.

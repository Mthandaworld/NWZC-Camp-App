“Do you see a man skilled in his work? He will stand before kings; he will not stand before mere men.” Proverbs 22:29

During the building of the sanctuary, God gave Moses specifications of exactly what He needed to be done. However, Moses wasn’t the one who was going to be doing the work. He was just going to mobilise the men who would do the work. These men had been specifically chosen by God Himself.

Read Exodus 31:1-11

·         What are the names of the people who would help in the work of the temple?

It is in the Bible for people to look for skilled labour, and it is in the Bible that God bestows skill on some people. Bezalel and his friends were skilled labourers, they had studied their work while in Egypt and they were further skilled by God Himself through His Holy Spirit. God skilled those in order to fulfil a task at hand. They did the work of the sanctuary as a team and worked together until it was completed.

Abraham and His Soldiers

When Lot parted ways with his uncle, he travelled for a while heading in the direction of Sodom. Eventually, he ended up in Sodom. However, while there, they were raided and Lot was taken captive as a slave. Someone managed to get the message to Abraham. Imagine his shock! His own nephew, captured and locked in chains! If you were Abraham, what would you have done?

Fortunately, Abraham was not worried, He had God on his side and he also had a group of skilled soldiers! Yes, he had 300 servants whom he had trained as soldiers (Genesis 14:14.) Imagine them marching towards the enemy singing “I am in the Lord’s army” or “Soldier soldier, march like a soldier” or even, “We are soldiers in the army” they went there and fought against the enemy and managed to free Lot.

Did you know that God has also given you a skill that you are yet to discover which you can use when you grow up?

What you have to do is to pray to God and ask Him to show you what you are supposed to be and God in His wisdom and love will show you exactly what you must do. There are some among you who will grow up to be business men, some of you will become mechanical and electrical engineers. Some will become doctors and nurses, some teachers and lecturers, some pastors and musicians, some will become bricklayers and architects.

God has bestowed a specific skill on you so that you might use it to help others, sustain yourself and work for Him.

·         Like Bezalel, your skill can help in building the house of God and even spreading the gospel.

·         Like Abraham, you can become a rich business man whose resources can be used to help others.

·         Like Joseph, who was a governor, you can sustain yourself and your family.

If you have something you want to be when you grow up, ask around if there is an older person who has the same job you want to do and let them help you with career guidance.

Reflections

1.       God has given each of us a special skill

2.       These skills will become our professions when we grow up

3.       We must prayerfully asked God about what He wants us to do when we grow up.

4.       Our skills will help others, help us in serving God and help us in sustaining ourselves and our families

 

Commitment

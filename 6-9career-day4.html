Introduction

Of all the people recorded in the New Testament, besides Jesus, the busiest person was perhaps Paul. He was previously called Saul but changed his name after his conversion. Before becoming a missionary, Paul had worked as a tentmaker. When he converted, Paul entered into ministry fulltime.

Into the lesson

Paul took many missionary journeys as he worked to spread the gospel. He worked tirelessly for the spreading of the gospel and visited many unentered areas to convert people to the Lord. He preached to prisoners, business people, philosophers, poets, soldiers and kings. However, despite having so much popularity as a preacher, he did not quit his job as a tentmaker. Wherever he visited, he would sometime look for fellow tentmakers and work with them (Acts 18:3).

 

This same Paul went further to talk about issues relating to working. He encouraged believes to be hardworking, not to be idle people following after gossip. Let us read some of his verses of encouragement:

·         1 Corinthians 4:12

·         Ephesians 4:28

·         1 Thessalonians 4:11

·         2 Thessalonians 3:8, 10, 11, 12

·         Titus 3:14

What do we learn from these verses?

Paul encourages everyone to be hardworking, he further tells his fellow believers that anyone who does not work must not eat!

Do you help at home or if you had lived during Paul’s time you would be one of those people who didn’t eat?

We must work at home, helping with chores and tasks. Our parents also depend on us to help them with work. There are some here who can’t wash their socks or their uniforms. When you go home, ask your parents/guardians to teach you how to wash your clothes. Some can’t even do the dishes or sweep the house. We must spread our own bed, if we sleep on the floor, we must learn to fold our own blankets.

Lesson Application: Know, Feel, Respond.

Just as Paul worked as a tentmaker while a missionary, we too can be at school and still be involved in God’s work. When at church, we can be asked to go for witnessing, to visit the sick, to sing for visitors or to do a Sabbath school presentation. Our school cannot affect what we can do for God.

Even when we grow up and become employed, we can still assist in God’s work by taking time off to go and work for God. Some of our parents/guardians sometimes leave work and go for Zundes or to preach at efforts. This is because they understand that working doesn’t remove them from their duty to their maker. We can also, like the women in Luke 8, help in the work of the gospel by our means.

Every Sabbath we collect offerings during Sabbath school and divine service, these offerings go a long way in aiding the work of God. We can be here in Zimbabwe but preach to people in Fuji Islands by giving offerings to God. Sometimes we can be asked to fund a particular project, for example, Zunde, Effort or a church building. These funds help in spreading the gospel. Sometimes we can be asked donate items that are needed by the less privileged. All these help in spreading the gospel and showing God’s love to those who need it.

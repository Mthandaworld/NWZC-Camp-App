Day Five: The Little Maid

 

Welcome

·         Greet the children and ensure that they are well seated and quite

·         Introduce yourself and tell the children the lesson you are going to be teaching.

·         Start with the theme song of the camp, “I shall not be moved” and have a prayer.

·         If you have a catchy slogan, introduce it and repeat it with the children, one such slogan is the theme itself, “Faithful Till the end-I shall not be moved!”

·         If the classes are small, allow your children to introduce themselves. If they are big, ensure that each child who leads in any activity whether singing, bible reading or raises hand to contribute, introduces themselves.

Lesson Objective: At the end of the lesson children should learn that they can continue to be shinning lights even in dire circumstances  

Recap of Previous Lesson, key highlights:

1.       We can work for God whilst doing our own work/school

2.       We can also do our own work while working for God

3.       Our offerings go a long way in spreading the gospel to others

4.       By participating in Sabbath school, singing, witnessing and health expos, we help in spreading the gospel

5.       Giving donations is another way of showing God’s love to the world

Key Take Aways

·         We have learnt through Adam, Eve and their children that it is important for us to have something that we do in life. Just as Cain and Abel were farmers, Cain as a tiller of the land and Abel as a shepherd, we too must have a certain task that we pursue.

 

·         We have also learnt that God has given each of us a skill that we can use to sustain ourselves, to serve others and to serve God. All we have to do is to pray and ask for guidance from God and He will reveal to us what we must do. If we have careers we have thought of, we must ask those who either know about them or those who are in the field so that we make an informed decision of what we want to do.

 

·         Paul’s lesson taught us that why we do our various tasks, we can also do God’s work. This means even when we eventually look for employment we must not forget our duty to God.

Introduction

Imagine that you are in class, you are not making noise but sitting quietly in your own chair. The teacher is absent so most kids are making noise. Then one of the popular kids takes a paper ball and throws it through the window. Unfortunately, the teacher is in the corridor, she sees the paper but doesn’t see who threw it. So she enters the class and asks who threw it. The popular kid quickly implicates you and because others like him/her more than you, they agree that it’s you. You try to clear your name but the teacher is having none of it and eventually punishes you for it.

The following morning you hear that the teacher is not feeling well and has been admitted at hospital, she fell ill the previous night. How would you feel about this kind of situation?

 

Into the lesson

Most of you would probably be very happy with what happened with the teacher because you think that maybe God punished her for what she did to you. Some can even thank God silently for giving that teacher exactly what she deserved. But is this how we should react when those who treat us badly are in trouble?

The Little Maid (2 Kings 6:1-2 Paraphrased)

A little girl was playing at home while her parents were close by, she was enjoying herself with no care in the world. Little did she know, the Syrians were coming to raid Israel and her village was part of the area that would be raided. All of a sudden mighty steel chariots were heard in the distance, the sound of people screaming was heard as dust rose like waves. “Syrians! Syrians! Run for your lives!”

The parents ran to get their little girl, but they were too late, the little girl was snatched away and she never got the chance to know what happened to her parents. She was thrown in a cage and the chariots continued they journey. They crossed river Jordan, she was going to a foreign place she had never been to. She had only heard of stories of Syria. Had she been going for a holiday, it would have been better, instead she was being taken against her will. Lonely, dejected and afraid, they carried her to Syria and she was given as a slave to the wife of the army commander whose name was Naaman.

She was tasked with cleaning the house, washing the clothes, cooking and other household chores. She didn’t like this place, she would rather go home and be with her parents. A few days passed then she heard whispers in the corridor, it seems the army commander wasn’t feeling well. She then heard her mistress saying it was a case of leprosy, and within Syria there was no one who could heal him.

The little girl remembered how in Israel a prophet had done so many wonders, she forgot her own problems and focused on the man who wasn’t feeling well. She had never seen him heal a person, but she was so sure that he could heal him too.

With confidence she told her mistress of the prophet in Israel, the mistress trusted this little girl and had faith in her confidence. She told her husband and immediately the husband made plans to go and be healed. When Naaman written, he had been healed!

Lesson Application: Know, Feel, Respond

Start by asking the children questions related to the story

·         Even in difficult situations where we feel we are being disadvantaged, we can still do well and be missionaries to others.

·         Jesus encourages us to be good even to those who are not good to us.

·         We must always be a light in every situation.

·         Even though the little maid was in a disadvantaged position, her presence was a blessing even to those who had captured her.

·         The little girl used her influence where she was working. We too can be influential in at our schools and even when we finally get employed

This little girl’s experience is a true testimony of being faithful till the end. She was not moved by her circumstances or situation but remained steadfast. She understood that her duty to others superseded her own comfort.

Those who pledge themselves to remain firm and not be moved by the world or different situations please stand up so that we pray.

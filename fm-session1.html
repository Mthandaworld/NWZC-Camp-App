The Importance of Marriage and Family

God established marriage and family (Gen 2:18-24). God intended the marriage between Adam and Eve to be the pattern for all marriages throughout the ages. Christ endorsed this original concept by saying “Have ye not read that he who made them at the beginning made them male and female … for this reason shall a man leave his father and mother and shall cleave to his wife, and they twain shall be one flesh (Matt 19:5; Mk 10:7-8; Eph 5:31). entrance of sin in the world adversely affected marriage and the quality of relationship between husband and wife including children.
When Adam and Eve sinned, they lost the oneness they had known with each other and with God (Gen 3:6-24). Their relationship was marked with guilt, shame, blame, and pain. Wherever sin permeates, its sad effects is felt. These include: alienation, desertion, unfaithfulness, neglect, abuse, violence, separation, divorce etc. It is primary purpose of the gospel to restore humanity to its original status and that includes the marriage covenant.

No church, community, or nation will rise higher than the spiritual condition of its families. God designed the family as the birthplace and residence of Christianity. It is the place where the knowledge, fear, and love of the Lord are taught by parents and learned by children. The family is where character is planted and grown. It is the place where civility and respect for others are nurtured and cultivated. Family reveals the real you and provides the right platform for self-discovery and self-examination.
The impact of family on children and the future generations is tremendously far reaching. Perhaps this could be the reason why the Psalmist says, “I will walk within my house in the integrity of my heart.” Ps 101:2. Ellen G. White says, “The restoration and uplifting of humanity begins in the home. ... the well-being of society, the success of the church, the prosperity of the nation, depend upon home influence,” Ministry of Healing, 349.

Functions of the Family

  • Spiritual grooming (Heb 11:7; Job 1:5)
  • Procreation -- family reproduction (Gen 1:23; Gen 2:24)
  • Emotional Support -- emotional relations, companionship (Gen 2:18)
  • Care -- providing security, stability and care of each other (1Tim 5:8)
  • Socialization -- teaching children to be obedient to the commands of God (Deut 6:4-8)
  • Transmission of Values -- passing on family values (Deut 6:4-8)
  • Identity -- establishing one’s identity, kinship and social status

The function of the family as an evangelism hub is highlighted by David and Vera Mace, “The Christian home is, in fact, by far the most powerful evangelizing agency in the world. Its evangelism, however, is not aggressive; it is persuasive. It proclaims its message not by words, but by deeds. It does not tell others what they should be; it shows them what they could be. By their gracious influence, Christian homes win more converts than all the preachers put together. Give us enough of them, and the world would soon be a Christian world; for the world’s life rises to the higher levels only as its homes do so.” In The Presence of God, 113.

STRONG FAMILIES

Noah’s family is an example of a strong family. The family lived at a time when the world was wholly given to corruption, moral depravity and violence (Gen 6 :1-8). Notice that Noah’s primary objective of building the ark was to save his family from the flood (Heb 11:7). Gen 7:7 records that Noah and his family went into the ark and were saved from the deluge. Noah prioritized the salvation of his family. We are living in the final days of this earth, as evidenced by a global repeat and recurrence of the sins of the antediluvian age (Lk 17:26-27). We need to pray for divine grace to create and sustain strong families able to resist the corruption of this world and be ready for the imminent glorious kingdom of the Lord Jesus Christ.

Below are the Characteristics of Strong Families

  1. Strong families express a great deal of appreciation to each other. 
  2. Parents are generous with praise.
  3. They give lots of positive messages and build each other up.  They frequently offer sincere compliments. 
  4. Family members regard each other warmly, positively, and give support to each other as individuals.
  5. Family members are encouraged to embrace their uniqueness and creative sides.
  6. Family members do not expect that life is completely routine or bland.
  7. Family members respect themselves and others
  8. Strong parental coalition -Cooperating not competing in parenting
  9. Clear boundaries
  10. Positive mental health
  11. Strong families spend a lot of time together.
  12. Family members work, play, and eat together. 
  13. They enjoy being together and structure their lifestyle to make the time together happen.
  14. Strong families have a high degree of commitment to the family as a group.
  15. The family unit is as important to its members as the individuals within the family. 
  16. Family members direct energy and time inward toward the family rather than outward to other interests.
  17. Strong families are committed to a spiritual lifestyle. 
  18. Their religion is personal as well as practical.  A spiritual sense of life gives family members a common belief and promotes family values.
  19. Strong families are able to deal with conflicts and band together in mutual support when bad times arise. 
  20. They keep a sense of balance by focusing on something positive about events, such as how much they care about each other.
  21. They offer strong support to the one in crisis.
  22. Family members are spontaneous.  They can express joy or sadness freely.
  23. Family members are free to interact with other family members in various ways
  24. They pray and worship together

One well-ordered, well-disciplined family tells more in behalf of Christianity than all the sermons that can be preached. {AH 32.1}

The greatest evidence of the power of Christianity that can be presented to the world is a well- ordered, well-disciplined family. This will recommend the truth as nothing else can, for it is a living witness of its practical power upon the heart. {AH 32.2}

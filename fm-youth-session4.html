 PARENTING FOR HEAVEN

What the Bible and Spirit of Prophecy says about Parenting

If God is to bless your marriage with children, it is important to know how He expects you raise them.

The Bible reminds us that “Children are a heritage from the Lord” (Psalm 127:3). And when these children come into the home, they should take their rightful place. Parents, therefore, who are given children by God have a consequent stewardship of far- reaching importance. ‘Train up a child in the way he should go, and when he is old he will not depart from it’ (Prov. 22:6). Children need, as the Scriptures indicate, ‘training in righteousness’ including necessary, detailed and practical ‘teaching’ ‘reproof and ‘correction’. It is for such training that the Scriptures are particularly ‘profitable’ because ‘inspired by God’ (see 2 Tim. 3:16). Children need to be taught moral discrimination so that later when they have to face life on their own they will know how ‘to refuse the evil and choose the good’ (see Isa. 7:16). To this end they need to become ‘skilled in the word of righteousness’; and to ‘have their faculties trained by practice to distinguish good from evil’ (see Heb. 5: 13,14).

Exod 20:12 “Honor your father and mother. Then you will live a long, full life in the land the LORD your God will give you.”

Don’t stop them! For the kingdom of Heaven belongs to such as these.” And he put his hands on their heads and blessed them before he left.

You may be evangelists in the home, ministers of grace to your children. {Child Guidance 479}

NB: Having children means more responsibility hence the more the children the more the responsibilty. There is nothing wrong for those contemplating marriage to decide on the size of their family beforehand so as to avoid bringing on earth children you cannot afford to raise in such ways as would bring honour and glory to God.

                        Positive Parenting Practices  

•         Parents should be generous with praise.

•         Give lots of positive messages to build up the child

•         Be readily there for their children

•         Teach positive thinking

•         Embrace their uniqueness and creative sides.

•         Travelling together

•         Eating together

•         Playing together

•         Going on holiday together

•         Support the child in the area of giftedness

•         Reinforce and teach personal hygiene

•         Teach them a self supporting skill

•         Teach how to handle pain and loss

•         Teach them to redeem time 

•         Teach them to deal with success and failure

•         To recognize essential versus important things

•         Teach them Financial management

•         Teach them about Courtship and marriage

•         Prioritize the family altar

•         Use redemptive methods of discipline

Family Worship

The altar of prayer and worship is the radar of the family. A family that prays together, stays together.

Some ways to make worship interesting at home

•     Avoid routine.

•     Have variety.

•     If children are able to read, let them lead out sometimes.

•     Use visual aids.

•     Have a song service.

•     Remember that they are family members and not a church audience.

•     Prepare for the lesson.

•     Let worship be short and interesting.

•      Allow room for interaction.

•     Let family members pray for each other.

•     Simplify concepts to their level.

•     Avoid bringing disciplinary issues during worship time.

•     Don’t conduct worship when you are angry.

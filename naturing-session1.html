Why Do People Leave the Church?

 

Research indicates that most former Adventists did not leave the Church because of doctrinal reasons. Some do, of course. But certainly not the majority. Most leave for one of the following four reasons:

1.      A conflict with another Church member or pastor.

2.      Discouragement over personal problems and guilt because of an inability to live up to biblical ideals.

3.      A growing disinterest in spiritual things because of a neglected devotional and prayer life.

4.      A perception that the church is no longer relevant to life and does not meet felt needs.

Discuss as a class the possible solutions to the above mentioned reasons. (10 minutes)

It is important to note that nurturing is key, when we talk about membership retention.


 

 

Meaning of nurturing.

According to Webster dictionary, nurture (verb) is defined as to educate, to bring or train up. Nurturing ministries endeavours to guarantee that a new member gains a solid base on which to establish faith and keep it strong and active.  

The Seventh day Adventist church seems to concentrate more on reaching out to the lost, winning them to Christ and adding them to church registers without a deliberate plan of nurturing them. One study has observed that in the past few decades Seventh-day Adventist membership records have reported a loss of about 40 percent of their membership. Even worse, more than half of those who leave are considered missing or unknown. Christian leaders attribute the current situation to faulty discipleship. The apostle Paul gave the following admonition to the elder of Ephesus:

Pay careful attention to yourselves and to all the flock, in which the Holy Spirit has made you overseers, to care for the church of God, which he obtained with his own blood (Acts 20:28). In the book of Peter we also read the following:

In order to prevent the membership lose the church has to serious come up with ways of nurturing new believers. Nurturing believers must not be left to chance or accident.

In the Adventist church a pastor is in charge of several congregations but he works with elders who are in charge of individual local churches. It is these elders (first elders) and other local church leaders who must ensure that the newly baptised are helped to find their way into the ministry of the church. How do they nurture these new believers?

·         Prepare new Adventist believers before baptism and integrate them into church life. Every congregation should have a baptismal class so that baptismal candidates are properly natured. The local church leadership train  new Adventist believers through baptismal classes, Bible studies; involve them into church life through small groups, Pathfinder, Youth Alive activities, prayer, social and other meetings. Those who are responsible for teaching baptismal classes should carefully prepare their lessons on fundamental beliefs. The lesson should have a good introduction, informative body and challenging conclusion. The lesson also needs relevant illustration that help the students apply whet they learn to their lives.

·         The lessons taught to new believers should emphasise the Adventist distinctives that comprise the 6s.

·         Salvation

·         Sabbath

·         State of the Dead

·         Second Coming

·         Stewardship

·         Sanctuary

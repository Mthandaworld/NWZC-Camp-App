Nurturing of Newly Baptised Members

by Pr L Tshuma and Pr K Moyo

S1 S2 S3 S4 S5

Session 2

·          Every congregation should have a fishers of man class. This is a special class for the newly baptised. It is in this class where the fundamental beliefs are reinforced to them. Furthermore, this class integrates new members into church life and ministries. The new members must be helped to discover and use their spiritual gifts within the church. They can even start using their gifts (Eph 4:11-15) in mid-week prayer meetings where there are non-threatening smaller groups.

Why is this class very important?
When should one graduate(move) from this class?

One can only move or graduate from this class when they have completed the bible doctrinal manual. (Not less than a year)

·         Every new believer should be given a spiritual parent who will foster their growth. The spiritual parents will then help the new converts grow to maturity through Bible study, prayer , meditation, fellowship, and involvement in service and mission.  Unfortunately some spiritual parents have not taken seriously their responsibility to disciple the new converts. The new converts have been left alone either to swim or to sink. This is spiritual baby-dumping. Some Adventist churches have left the nurturing of new convert to the fishers of man class which meets for lessons once a week on the Sabbath. The lessons held on Sabbath are not adequate to make disciples. Many local church leaders seem to think that more lessons on fundamental beliefs will disciple people. In order to produce genuine disciples the church needs more that teaching Bible lessons. The new convert should learn the Bible stories and verses. The new converts should also learn to apply the knowledge of the Bible in real life as they observe how their spiritual parents live out the Christian life. The new believers should also observe how their mentors struggle to earn a living in an economically unstable environment. Pauls mentees must have observed him as he went from house to house conducting Bible studies (Acts 20:20) during the day. They must have observed how he would make tents at night (1 Thessalonians 1:9-10) which were later sold. They also noted that he refused financial assistance. In that way he was different from those who preached for gain.

·         Since the highest % of membership loss is through missing or members whose whereabouts is not known, Church clerks, with the assistance of elders, should teach the newly converts the importance of membership transferee. There are many, who when they relocate do not take the necessary steps to apply for their names, simple because they do not know.  Church Manual 57-61, 87.

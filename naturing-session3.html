How can you apply these keys in your church?

 

Key 1. Mentor or guardian: 1Timothy 1:18

As mentioned earlier, new members will not survive if they are left alone. Baptism symbolizes a new spiritual birth, not a spiritual maturity. Seasoned Church members have the responsibility for mentoring new converts. This may be accomplished when we show:

-          -Genuine concern for the new believer’s spiritual walk.

-          -sharing of testimonies from the mentors own spiritual journey.

-          -Giving Adventists books.

-          -sending them massages of encouragement.

-          -visiting and praying with them in their homes during the week following their baptism.

-          -sharing a meal with new believers.

-          -sympathize with them when they lose their beloved ones.

What other activities can be done to mentor new believers?

“After individuals have been converted to the truth, they need to be looked after. ... These newly converted ones need nursing, watchful attention, help, and encouragement. These should not be left alone, a prey to Satan’s most powerful temptations; they need to be educated regarding their duties, to be kindly dealt with, to be led along, and to be visited and prayed with.” (Evangelism, p. 337)

Key 2: New Church Friends: Proverbs 17:17, Proverbs 18:24

Ellen White counsels:

“Those who have newly come to the faith should be patiently and tenderly dealt with, and it is the duty of the older members of the church to devise ways and means to provide help and sympathy and instruction for those who have conscientiously withdrawn from other churches. The church has a special responsibility laid upon her to attend to these souls who have followed the first rays of light they have received; and if the members of the church neglect this duty, they will be unfaithful to the trust that God has given them.” (Evangelism, p.351)

According to Ellen White what keeps people in church membership is the way they are treated after they join church membership. Perhaps the best way of leading people to discipleship is using the best methods of discipling them towards conversion and also finding the best way of nurturing them in the faith after conversion. In order to reduce drop-outs both pre-conversion and post-conversion disciple making must be perfected.

Bruce Johnston argues that the new believers should know six or eight people in the church within the first six months of membership. Those who knew people stay in the church; those who do not, cannot stay.”

Key 3: Ongoing instruction. Exodus 18:20

According to the Bible, the Israelites were baptized when they crossed the red sea. 1 Corint 10:2. God instructed Moses, to teach these newly converts His laws and ordinances. According to Exodus18:20, spiritual growth happens when new converts do three things:

1.      Read His laws

2.      Put into practice what they read.. ‘Show them the way they must walk’

3.      Teach others what they have learnt. ‘..the work they must do.’

                  

   The same principle is also found in the book of Ezra.   

Ezra 7:10

For Ezra had prepared his heart to seek the law of the Lord, and to do it, and to teach in Israel statutes and judgments.

 In His earthly ministry, Jesus also taught about these three steps:

Luke 10:26 ‘… How readest thou.’

Luke 10: 28 ‘Do this and thou shalt live.’

Matthew 28:19 ‘Go and teach all nations’

 

From the writings of E G White we read the following about the importance of ongoing instruction.    

“New converts will need to be instructed by faithful teachers of God’s Word that they may increase in a knowledge and love of the truth and may grow to the full stature of men and women in Christ Jesus.”  Evangelism, p. 337

Key 5: Training and Involvement in Ministry: Joshua 10:36, Luke 19:13

 

“When souls are converted, set them to work at once. And as they labor according to their ability, they will grow stronger. It is by meeting opposing influences that we become confirmed in the faith.” (Evangelism, p. 355)

 

It is important that each member be involved in mission and ministry at church as regular as possible. Each member should hold the work of Christ according to their ability and potential capabilities.

 

How long should one take before being given something to do at church?

 

The above quotation does not encourage us to hurry newly baptized members into office. The emphasis is that they should be involved in the activities of the Church such as taking part in the Sabbath School or offering a prayer during service or part of a witnessing team. They should not be spectators who are merely watching us doing our own things.

 

“In many places we meet men who have been hurried into responsible positions as elders of the church when they are not qualified for such a position. They have not proper government over themselves. Their influence is not good. The church is in trouble continually in consequence of the defective character of the leader. Hands have been laid too suddenly upon these men.”—4T 406, 407. (See also 5T 617 and 1 Tim. 5:22.) Church Manual Pages 75,76.

 

“Let all be taught how to work. Especially should those who are newly come to the faith be educated to become laborers together with God.” (Christian Service, p. 69)

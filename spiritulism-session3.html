SESSION 3: EXAMPLE OF SPIRITUALISM IN THE BIBLE

 

Spiritualism, when found among non-believers is not a problem at all. The challenge is that it is a sin quite common amongst God’s people. Consciously or unconsciously, God’s people often fall into the snares of Satan through spiritualism.

 

King Saul and the witch of Endor (1 Samuel 28:1-25)

 

Read the story of King Saul and the spirit medium of Endor. It is one of the best examples of how God’s people fall for spiritualism. We believe that the story is in the Bible for a reason (1 Corinthians 10:11).

 

·         Saul was well aware of God’s direct prohibition against consulting the dead or doing anything for their sake, (Leviticus 19:31; Lev 20:27; Deuteronomy 18:9-14).

·         At one point, in obedience to the Lord, King Saul had put the mediums and the spirits out of the land (1 Samuel 28:3).

·         He became afraid of the armies of the Philistines (28:5).

·         When he inquired of the Lord, the Lord did not answer him either by dreams or by Urim or by the prophets (28:6).

·         He resorted to consulting a spirit medium.

·         He had to disguise himself in order to approach the woman.

·         He requested for Samuel to be brought up.

·         The woman perceived that it was Saul inquiring from her

·         The woman saw a spirit ascending out of the earth

·         Saul perceived that it was Samuel or his Spirit ascending out of the earth

·         The spirit perceived to be Samuel, rebuked Saul for disobeying the Lord, and threatened him that Israel would be defeated in war, and that, “tomorrow you and your sons will be with me.”

 

As you analyze this story, answer the following questions:

1.      Why did God prohibit the practice of consulting the dead

2.      What drives people to consult the dead?

3.      Who/what appeared to the woman and the king, that they perceived as Samuel?

4.      The rebuke given to Saul was true and the prediction seemed to be ‘true’. What was wrong with that ‘truth’?

To answer these questions, we can look at the next lesson on the state of the dead.

 

Modern Spiritualism and Syncretism

 

Syncretism is combining elements from different (even conflicting) religious or spiritual traditions. One of the dangers of syncretism in matters of faith is that it brings confusion and inconsistency. Many Christian denominations attempt to embrace spiritualism as a normal pursuit which is in harmony with biblical standards. Others practice these things separately.

 

What examples can you give of modern spiritualism?

SESSION 4: BIBLE TRUTH ON THE STATE OF THE DEAD

 

The basic and yet foundational truth about the dead is in Ecclesiastes 9:5, “For the dead know not anything.”

This simply means that there is no consciousness in death.: no thinking, no hearing, no seeing, no speaking, no moving.” The dead know NOTHING. The following facts explain this further:

 

1.      At creation man was formed as an indivisible unit of the body and the breath of life, “And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his of breath of life, and man became a living being.” Genesis 2:7.

2.      There is no living soul within a living person. The breath of life is a breath to give life. Man became a living soul.

3.      Death, is described as the reverse of the creation of man, “…till you return to the ground, for out of it you were taken; for dust you are, and to dust you shall return,” (Genesis 3:19).

4.      At death, the body and the breath of life separate. One without the other is not a living thing. God retains the power to give life (Ecclesiastes 12:7).

5.      The next time they become alive is when God resurrects the dead. The resurrection of the dead is the hope of all Christian believers and those who die in Christ will rise first ahead of the wicked. There will be two resurrections, the first for the righteous and the second and last one for the wicked. (John 5:18-29; 1 Corinthians 15:51-58; 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18).

6.      The resurrection of the bible is the bodily resurrection, not some intangible spirit. When Jesus rose from the dead, He could be touched and He could eat (Luke 24:39-43).

7.      Between the moment of death and the moment of resurrection, the dead are unconscious, not knowing anything. (Ecclesiastes 9:5).

8.      It is this period between death and resurrection that Satan and his angels come in, and personate the dead, claiming that the dead are not really dead. They work with all their power, in so many ways to prove that they are indeed, the departed ones. It is also within this period between death and resurrection that the living attempt to reach out to the dead. Demons take advantage of their ignorance and unbelief in the word of God, and responds to their efforts by claiming to be the dead whom they are seeking. All things, whether good or bad attributed to the dead are actually the works of the evil angels.

9.      Consulting or interacting with the so called ‘dead’ is direct interaction with the evil angels themselves. It is surrendering to their control and it amounts to worshipping the devils.

10.  The bible teaches people to trust in God and not to seek help from the dead (Isaiah 8:19).

11.  Consulting evil powers is defiling and corrupting.

 

 

Important Notes

 

As we learn the truth about the state of the dead, the following facts respond to questions that often arise in many minds:

 

·         The bible records that there are individuals who were translated and went to heaven without dying. Enoch was taken to heaven without seeing death after he had walked with God for 300 years (Genesis 5:24; Hebrews 11:5). Elijah the prophet was taken to heaven (2 Kings 2:11).

·         At His second coming, Jesus will find some alive. The dead saints will be raised and the living saints will be translated. Both groups will be glorified, meaning, they will obtain a body that is immortal, incorruptible. “We shall not all sleep but we shall all be changed,” (1 Corinthians 15:51).   

 

·         The bible also records that there are saints who were resurrected and taken to heaven.

 

·         Moses died and was buried (Genesis 34:5-6), but Michael the Archangel’s dispute with the devil over Moses’ body in which the devil was rebukes, suggests that at that moment, Moses was raised to life by Christ (Jude 1:9). This is evidenced by Moses’ appearance with Elijah on the Mount of Transfiguration, as they discussed with Jesus (Matthew 17:1-4).

 

·         There is a group of saints who were raised from the dead when Christ died on the cross, who were seen by many in Jerusalem when Jesus rose from the dead (Matthew 27:51-53). It is possible that these were taken to heaven by Jesus at his ascension. Ellen White says that, “those who were raised at Christ's resurrection got possession of eternal life and then rose with Him to the heavens as evidence of His triumph over death and the grave.” From heaven With Love, page 523.

 

·         The twenty-four elders seen in Revelation 4; 5; 7; 11; 14; 19, must be saints redeemed from the earth, possibly having been resurrected from the dead, perhaps part of the saints mentioned above. Their kind of crown they have are crowns of victory (Revelation 4:10) not just royal crowns. They confess in their new song that they were redeemed by the blood of the lamb that was slain (Revelation 5:9). At some point these saints were redeemed from the earth.  

 

·         The wicked dead will also be raised to life, a thousand years after the saints would have been resurrected (Revelation 20:5-6). They are raised to receive the judgment (Revelation 20:12-14) and they are all thrown into the lake of fire (Revelation 20:14-15) to join the devil and his angels in their destruction (Matthew 25:41).

 

·         The fire that will destroy the devil and his angels, and all the wicked people will have an eternal effect. The burning will take place until they are all completely consumed to extinction. The fires will not burn forever and ever. It is also one of Satan’s lies that God will burn the wicked continually forever. It is the effects of the fire that will be everlasting, not the flames themselves. In this destruction, God shows His mercy by removing the devil and sinners from existence forever.
